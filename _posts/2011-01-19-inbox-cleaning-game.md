When I was young, my mom always tried to turn cleaning up into a game.

"Pick up all of the RED toys!"

"Let's see who can clean up the dress-up clothes the fastest?"

And so on and so forth.

Now, as an adult, my 'playroom' is now a virtual one. It's called my inbox. And it is still messy-sometimes overwhelmingly so.

And I'm pretty sure I'm not alone (right?)

I imagine it is why there are a billion theories floating around about email communications and efficiency, and countless ideas on how to achieve "inbox 0."

But what if cleaning out your inbox was an interactive game? A free one at that?

Would you be more inclined to clean up your digital act?

Perhaps.

That's what the people over at [Baydin](<http://emailgame.baydin.com/about.html>) are hoping, at least.

They've created a fun, simple [Email Game](<http://emailgame.baydin.com/ >). And it works like this:

![](https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/Picture-4.png)

Enter your email address, click play, and watch the game help you sort through your inbox. The faster you work-and the more you take action (responding, deleting, archiving) the more points you get.

When I first tried it my blood pressure was quickly escalating but fear not, you are in complete control of what happens to your emails.

The only downside I noticed is that if you are already an archiver like me, the exercises felt a little pointless, as it just took me through the few emails left in my inbox awaiting action instead of addressing the thousands of worthless ones I have in my archived folder.

Try it, what do you have to lose?

They are looking for feedback via twitter.com/@[emailgame](<http://twitter.com/emailgame>) and we'd love to hear comments on what you think of the game here at the blog!
