If you're anything like me, December is a tough month to stay focused. There is college basketball, football bowl games, family visiting, cookies to bake, gifts to buy and wrap and on top of it all WORK. Now that we're all out of college and don't get a month to relax over the holidays it is important not to waste this last month of the year.

I consider myself a master planner in all aspects of life, and have decided to share my list of five ways I plan to stay productive the rest of December and, after eating myself into a coma, early January.

1\. **Keep Scheduling Meetings**  
I'm at work, and I assume you are too, so let's get together and talk about the past year and look at next year's opportunities. There's no rule that all business has to stop leading up to and right after the Holidays. Plus, it's always good to talk to people that don't have embarrassing childhood stories about you to tell.

2\. **Make a List (and check it twice)**  
Santa is on to something with this. It's been helpful to me to have a list of tasks at work and things to get done after 5 pm. It allows me to budget my time and make sure that I'm getting everything done.

3\. **Set Goals for December**  
Even though business quiets down, see what tangible measurable goals you can try and still accomplish. Try digging through the "someday" folder and tackle some of those housekeeping tasks that you didn't have time for in August. Whether it's updating your company profile on linked in, editing your sales process or mission statement or brainstorming on a new social media communication plan, there's something somewhere that once upon a time you wish you had the time for. And, now you do!

4\. **Plan for 2011**  
If your clients, prospects or friends are already on vacation, get your notes and thoughts in order to jump start the year, after you wake up from your food-induced slumber of course. Set your sights high by looking through your lists and determining what can be continued after the new year.

5.**Relax  
**There are many opportunities for stress to creep in, and usually that leads to less productivity at work, or snapping at family members when you get home. Avoid the cookie table at work so you can spend time on numbers 1-4 and get prepared to have a wonderful time with friends and family.

Now that this has made itself to the Centresource blog, I have to keep to my own set of rules. I'll be back in 2011 to tell you how I've done, and share some New Year's Resolutions. Happy Holidays!
