With the recent release of Mac OS X Tiger, I've heard [people herald the end of the folder](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050612232230/http://www.wired.com/news/mac/0,2125,67774,00.html>). While I can appreciate the sentiments of those that love Desktop search and all the new doors it opens, let me state emphatically that folders are not in danger of disappearing anytime soon. The reason? Half of us can't remember what we're even looking for!

When you need something on your machine, it doesn't always start with, "Hmmm... where did I place file XYZ.123". When its not, using a desktop search utility loses some of its appeal.

Originally, I was going to give examples using pictures or music - but I've seen some comment that specific programs like iTunes & iPhoto basically take the place of folders by organizing your media in a seamless environment.

As such, I'll describe the need for folders in a slightly more complicated fashion. Let's say I have a project that I'm working on. This project requires a typed word document, a Power Point presentation, 2 audio files that were used in the presentation, 5 pictures I inserted into the word document, and to top it off... a PDF file that is the final product of all the work combined.... and why not toss in a make shift Website for my final presentation.

Without folders, Desktop search isn't going to help me find all the materials originally associated with that project. And that's the true kicker... **ASSOCIATION!** Its for this reason that Desktop Search won't completely kill the folder for a while - at least not until we figure out an easy way to deal with file association.

_Put that in your pipe and smoke it..._
