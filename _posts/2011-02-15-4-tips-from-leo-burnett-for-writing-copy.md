I have a sticky note on my desk that says, "Copy should make people think, laugh, share."

Now, that is certainly not the only content rule I stick to by any means, but in order to compel and convert, it's undeniable that you must find a way to engage your audience.

But how? How do you make your copy work for you?

Acclaimed advertising mogul, [Leo Burnett](<http://leoburnett.com/>)-creator of such legendary campaigns as the [Jolly Green Giant](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jolly_Green_Giant> "Jolly Green Giant"), the [Marlboro Man](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marlboro_Man> "Marlboro Man"), [Toucan Sam](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toucan_Sam> "Toucan Sam"), [Pillsbury Doughboy](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pillsbury_Doughboy> "Pillsbury Doughboy"), and [Tony the Tiger](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_the_Tiger> "Tony the Tiger")- had 4 rules for effective copy writing that are worth reading.

  1. Make it Simple
  2. Make it Memorable
  3. Make it Inviting to Look At
  4. Make it Fun to Read



Check out [this great article](<http://thewritingbase.com/the-leo-burnett-guide-to-writing-web-copy>) for the full description of these tips!

What rules do you live by for creating compelling content?
