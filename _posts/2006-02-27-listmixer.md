Time for a post about the killer app du jour: [Listmixer](<http://listmixer.com/>).

By the looks of it, it probably only took Sid Stewart maybe an evening of programming to whip up this site's functionality: proof of the value of a good idea and a simple, clean interface. Listmixer basically gives you disposable bookmarks. They expire after 30 days, allow quick tagging, and an RSS interface.

For a long time I've wanted some quick, easy way to keep track of conversational threads on blogs I am involved in, but I didn't want to clutter up my [actual bookmarks](<https://web.archive.org/web/20060310190730/http://del.icio.us/cwage/>) with this very temporary stuff. Listmixer looks to be the perfect answer. The only thing that would make it better is if it had a way to track updates to a post (the content, or even better, to comments, via the comments RSS feeds).

But this tool is a testament to the maxim of successful product development: do one thing, and do it well.
