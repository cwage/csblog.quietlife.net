date: 2005-09-28

Tonight... I had the single most frustrating customer support help I've ever had - and I thought I'd share it as an example of what NOT to do when providing 'self help' on your website.

A few weeks ago, I downloaded Magic The Gathering Online (MTGO) from www.wizards.com. After playing the demo a few times, one of their volunteers offered me a free upgraded account. I followed her instructions and spent 20 minutes signing away my personal information for a silly game.

Once I completed my registration, I received an email that gave me my new login and password. Upon trying it, it failed repeatedly. She said it takes time for the server and the application to 'synch' - so I waited patiently. A few days later, I tried again... only to get the same failure. I reviewed my original emails and saw that I could email tech support at techsupport@wizards.com.

I send an email to them and lay out my problem. A few days later, I try again... no dice. So I send another email. Rinse and repeat, rinse and repeat... I finally send 3 emails and the last is asking why they won't respond.

So naturally, I visit the site. Their wonderful (joke) support site has a way to contact them for help - IF YOU REGISTER. That's right, you have to register (in this case, again) to actually email them anything... anything period.

So, I visit their parent organization - Hasbro.com. It was linked from the Wizards site. I think, "Surely a large corporation like Hasbro will help me out. The small Wizards division just has their website/support processes messed up. I visit the 'contact us' page and I fill out the simple help form - it even allows me to select 'Wizards of the Coast' products in my drop downs.

When I click submit, my temper starts to flare. Instead of asking me to register BEFORE I type my long messages (like on Wizards.com), Hasbro lets me type my long message, click submit, and then asks me to 'complete' my registration. Finally, I give in and fill out their registration form with all sorts of fake data (like 123 Do Not Send Mail) and click submit. Whew, finally registered.

This was the straw that broke the camels back:  
"Hi Privacy, Thank you for contacting Hasbro Inc. We are sorry but we are unable to assist you with your inquiry as Wizards of the Coast handles this product. Please visit their web site at http://www.wizards.com. You may find the answer to your question in their FAQ section. If you do not find the information you require you may call them at 800-324-6496 or e-mail them at custserv@wizards.com. Thanks again for contacting us. We appreciate your interest."

Why in the world would they let me select the Wizards product from their submission form if they weren't going to help me. I wish one thousand curses on the Hasbro and Wizards webmasters.

Take note people - do not put your people through such a frustrating experience.
