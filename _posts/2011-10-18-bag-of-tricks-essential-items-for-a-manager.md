**Top 5 "Must Have" items for a manager**

**1\. Canned Responses** \- If you aren't a Gmail user yet, shame on you. If you're a Gmail user and haven't discovered canned responses yet, double shame!! About 30 times a week I write the same email to either customers, staff or peers. You know you type a phrase too often when your fingers move so fast your mind doesn't even process what you are typing! Canned responses are an amazing tool that allow you t compose pre-saved content items and then insert them into your emails. Life changing! I use them for reports to staff and management, customer surveys and job applicant responses. Perhaps not the nicest thing I've ever done, but each way to make me more efficient leads to better outcomes for all - thus the ends justifies the means I think!

**2\. Google Docs** \- I know there have been thousands of blogs praising the value of Google Docs but I'll tell you - they are a game changer when it comes to management. I use spreadsheets to track much of my sales teams weekly reports. These reports are visible to both the employee and management. Each change is saved a revision eliminating the he-said, she-said world of document versioning. I use word processing documents to record policies, document templates and annual strategies for out entire team in Google docs. Having these publicly visible makes presentations, team meetings and peer-based accountability a breeze. 

**3\. Tungle** \- ([www.tungle.me](<http://www.tungle.me/>))This tool has changed my life! You know that dozens of useless emails per week that go back and forth - they sound something like this..."I'd like to set a meeting for you around 2:00 next Thursday, would that work"

"I'm not free Thursday...how about Tuesday instead at 2:00?" 

"Close, how about 3:30 instead?"

Imagine a world where you could see each others calendars, find available times and then request a meeting at that time. Amazing! No more back and forth! One request - one meeting. Hours saved each month!!!

**4\. Remember the Milk** \- ([www.rememberthemilk.com](<http://www.rememberthemilk.com/>)) All the time I will assign a task to someone and tell them to let me know when it's done. This only works if they actually [![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/rememberthemilkcartonpt9-289x300.png)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/rememberthemilkcartonpt9.png>)track it and then tell me when it's complete. Anytime I assign something to someone that is a short task (aka, not one I track in my CRM) I add it to my Remember the Milk task list. It's great to walk up to someone months later and ask them if they ever got XYZ complete. I think half of my team thinks I have a super strong memory when in actually RTM keeps me on point. It's also great to keep track of personal things you need to remember. Every night as I lay down my mind starts to work overtime thinking through a litany of things I haven't written down anywhere that I have to get done. I use to lay awake worrying about them...now I just grab my phone and open up my RTM app, jot it down and sleep easy! Also, don't go for the free option, instead pay the bit extra for the professional. 

**5\. Extra 45 minutes** \- This is one I'm still really struggling with. I've learned that most of my meetings run about one hour in length. I try to build in travel time but if my meetings are in the CS offices I forget that as I step out of a meeting I will have a line of people waiting to ask me questions. Thus, by the time I've answered the questions, I am down to less than 30 minutes travel time. This is a sure fire way to be late to meetings! The old rule of thumb was 30 minutes between meetings for me, but I've raised it to 45 minutes now to account for the questions I'll be asked!

So there you have it! The first set of top 5 essential tools for a manager!!! I hope you enjoyed and there's more to come.
