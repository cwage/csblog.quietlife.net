Well, Thursday is D-Day for Win2k's transition to its "extended" support phase. While I [noted](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050522212019/http://blog.centresource.com/2005/04/25/good-night-sleep-tight/>) this previously, I didn't bother to do the research on what this means as far as a timeline for upgrading. This is what Microsoft has to say on their [bulletin](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050630234136/http://www.microsoft.com/windows2000/server/evaluation/news/bulletins/extendedsupport.asp>) for Windows 2000's transition to its "Extended phase" of support:

> Microsoft is _not_ ending support for Windows 2000. During the Extended Support phase, Microsoft continues to provide security hot fixes and paid support but no longer provides complimentary support options, design change requests, and non-security hotfixes. 

This is good news -- it means security updates will continue to be provided for the duration of the extended support phase, which according to the [Product LifeCycle Dates](<http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx?scid=fh;\[ln\];LifeWin>) is until June 30th, 2010.
