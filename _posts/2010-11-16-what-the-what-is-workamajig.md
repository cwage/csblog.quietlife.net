Interactive Agencies are known for pushing the technological envelope in almost every way. But if you asked them how they were managing their projects you might be surprised at the archaic answers. Excel spreadsheets to handle timeline & budget, invoicing through an outdated version of Quickbooks, and designs shared by zipped emails. Four years ago this described us well. Our ambition and creativity were hampered by our lack of consistent process. Then we met [Workamajig](<http://www.creative-manager.com/>). 

WMJ is the Swiss Army Knife of project management software for the creative firm. It encompasses all aspects of your day to day operations from scheduling details, to scope, to invoicing. In one glance you can get a report on how many (if any) overbudget projects you have, who is behind on their timesheets, and what your percentage of profitability is for Quarter 3. It allows a scattered group of left brained mavens manage the books wisely. 

Over the next few blogs I'll post some experiences we've had with WMJ and how we are using it to focus ourselves. Be forewarned, if you do decide to start using WMJ the frontend work in setting everything up correctly is laborious. The benefits of an application this size are in direct connection to the quality of the data you put in. That said, ask yourself these three questions, "Which service lines had the highest profit margin last quarter? How many projects did we deliver on time in 2010? Are all of our staff fully booked for the next 4 weeks?" If you answered 'I don't know', read on.
