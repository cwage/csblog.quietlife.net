The best password is an obscure hard-to-guess-or-brute-force password that only exists in your head. However, these sorts of passwords are hard to come by and inconvenient for your average user.

The worst password of course is an easy-to-remember-and-guess password. However, these are the most common, as users frequently gravitate towards these simple passwords, encouraged by the myth that writing down your password is a cardinal sin.

It's not. [Write down your password.](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050620004945/http://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2005/06/write_down_your.html>) The chances of someone managing to get ahold of a piece of paper tucked away in your wallet are much less than the chances of your password, "tree", being compromised by brute-force. Further, when you lose your wallet and hence your password, you **know** that you lost it, and hence can simply change your password(s). You will not have this luxury with a brute-forced password until it's already too late.

So, relax. If you can't remember a suitably strong password, write it down and keep it somewhere safe (that is, **not** on a sticky-note on your monitor.)
