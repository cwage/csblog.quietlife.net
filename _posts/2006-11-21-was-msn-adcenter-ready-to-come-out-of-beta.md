I have to say that my experience with Microsoft AdCenter was rather disappointing. 

Although MSN AdCenter has attempted to mirror strong account features that are provided by Google and Yahoo!, they were not implemented on a reliable scale. I feel that  
human factor was ignored during the development of MSN AdCenter. Even though the "full version" of MSN AdCenter was rolled out during September of 2006, it still SCREAMS beta. 

**Account Navigation**  
Advertisers are not able to use the back button within any given browser without being forced to login again. 

Google for instance may not submit an ad group if the back button is used on the same page as the final submission but doesn't require having to login again. It's quirky, but I can live with it. On the other hand, being forced to log back in is on the same scale as being timed out because you didn't use your mouse for 3 minutes. 

**Customer Service**  
My experience has lead me to believe that MSN support representatives lack the knowledge base to answer basic questions of PPC advertising. Even questions in regards to account navigation lead to a barrage of apologies and 3-8 minute hold times as they would seek advice from their superiors. 

There was an instance when the GEO Targeting feature "broke" within our account and brought unwanted traffic from opposite coasts and burned up our monthly budget. I was immediately blamed for the feature being broken because I was not aware that their account structure cannot handle multiple changes within close time periods. 

**Budget Feature**  
This was one of the most disappointing features within the MSN AdCenter account structure. There is a quirk within this feature that doesn't allow the modification of a paused campaign budget without running the risk of activating the account. Unaware of this quirk, I modified the budget of a paused AdCenter campaign and it caused it to activate the following day and we were stuck with the charges. 

The MSN AdCenter representatives stated that the budget feature should protect from inadvertently activating paused campaigns. When I requested a refund I was told that the modification of the budget was an "acceptance" of charges on my part and I would not be allowed a refund. I was thankfully able persuade the higher powers to provide a refund after almost three weeks of back and forth arguing. 

**Click Fraud**  
We received large surges of traffic from California between 1 and 5am for niche keywords and phrases that directly pertained to Nashville. This immediately raised a red flag for me and I quickly contacted the MSN AdCenter in regards to this matter. Unlike other times, I was fortunately not blamed for this incident but was not provided with any intelligent answer or solution to my concern. 

**Our Conclusion**  
I have been lead to the conclusion that MSN AdCenter is not the best marketing solution to provide to our clients and cannot be trusted to provide a reliable return on investment. CentreSource will only pursue marketing efforts with MSN AdCenter after substantial improvements are made within their account structure or otherwise specifically requested by our clients.
