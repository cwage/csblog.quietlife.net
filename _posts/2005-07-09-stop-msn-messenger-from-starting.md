I recently updated my browser and now I'm the lucky winner of a persistent start-up buddy, MSN Messenger 7.0. I was annoyed that MSN messenger even installed itself on my computer in the first place - I wanted the browser software, not MSN Messenger 7.0. But alas, the little icon appeared in my task bar upon restart. As if that weren't enough, a gaudy window appears with a button 'Sign-In'. So, to clarify: Microsoft forced me to accept the MSN Messenger, they added it to my start-up, and they made it pop-up in my face every time. The icing on the cake: I can't use 'run: msconfig' to disable it - it bypasses these settings and starts itself up again. So, here's what you must do to stop it:

MSN Messenger 7.0  
1) When the gaudy window appears, select 'Tools -> Options...' from the menu  
2) When the new split window appears, choose 'General' on the left side  
3) Uncheck 'Automatically run Messenger when I log on to Windows'

** Note: If you use the MSN Browser, messenger automatically starts - whether you do the above steps or not.
