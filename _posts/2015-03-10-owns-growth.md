![Who Owns Growth?](/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Screen-Shot-2015-01-27-at-1.15.48-PM-300x195.png)Year after year, businesses claim that growth is the most mission-critical, top priority task at hand for them. "Growth" may fall into one of several buckets for a business: growing market share, growing margins, or actually expanding the market you serve (growing customer base).

But this begs the question: who is in charge of growth within an organization? Is it the Sales Team? Marketing? How about building a product or service so fantastic that it will simply attract customers on it's own?

Here's some food for thought: what if growth isn't just one department's responsibility? What if the ultimate growth comes from having a unified growth strategy across sales, marketing, and product/service teams? As they say, a rising tide lifts all boats.

When the walls between internal teams subside, the communication lines are opened, and everyone is working toward the same goal -- an organization is able to tap into it's full growth potential. And that means greater opportunities for its employees, it's stakeholders, and ultimately - its customers.

![Raise the tide](/wp-content/uploads/2015/03/Screen-Shot-2015-01-27-at-1.34.39-PM-300x149.png)Every business should be able to answer the question of, Who Owns Growth?  
And whoever that person or people are - need to focus 100% of their energy on figuring out ways to raise the tide across Sales, Marketing, and Service/Product.
