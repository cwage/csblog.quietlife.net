What a week!

We launched a new site this week for a great cause called [The Two Futures Project -- an organization is focused on nuclear abolition. ](<https://web.archive.org/web/20090415193911/http://www.twofuturesproject.org/>)

The Two Futures Project was one of those great projects in which everyone on the team -- client included -- was passionate about the idea and about what interactive could do for the cause.

In our Strategy session, we focused on developing meaningful ways that users could take action in a cause that seems immovable from an individuals perspective (it's not like you can disarm the world's nukes by switching light bulbs). Our strategy aimed at on using the web to leverage the user's voice. As a result, our Phase 1 includes a registration process designed to automate the process of writing letters to government officials. We integrated social networking tools to help the user spread the word through social networks. And we developed a "Make the Case" section that invites users to share their own YouTube videos offering their "30 second pitch" for why nuclear disarmament is necessary.

We're most proud of our multimedia work on this one. We identified during Strategy that an emotional topic such as this needs media to highlight the importance of the movement -- one that could be easily shared between users and multi-purposed as the movement grows into speaking engagements, community meetings, and more. Our multimedia department put the video together, and we're extremely proud of the results.

Finally, we talked social media -- and days after launch we're already seeing results. Tyler Wigg-Stevenson, the director of the Two Futures Project, launched his organization and site in conjunction with this weeks' Q Conference. On Monday after his speech, there was palpable buzz about Two Futures on Twitter -- we've already seen hundreds of Twitter posts featuring this brand new cause. This corresponds to traffic: as of yesterday, Twitter and Facebook combined account for about 32% of the site's traffic, lagging just behind direct visits as Q Conference attendees type the URL directly into their browser.

We're thrilled to see that the press machine is moving quickly for the Two Futures Project -- [three days in and the movement is already featured on the Huffington Post!](<https://web.archive.org/web/20090503122134/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/rev-tyler-wiggstevenson/going-nuclear-young-chris_b_193135.html>)

Tyler thanked us in the best way possible: by posting a shout-out in our digital world (on his [@2FP Twitter account](<https://web.archive.org/web/20110206051242/http://www.twitter.com/2fp>)). See the Two Futures team below with the best Thank You note we could imagine. Bigger thanks to you Tyler -- we're so glad to be a part of this.

Now, check out [www.TwoFuturesProject.org](<https://web.archive.org/web/20090415193911/http://www.twofuturesproject.org/>) and join the movement!
