Last night CentreSource hosted the [The Nashville Ruby on Rails / Agile Software Meetup Group](<http://www.meetup.com/nashvilleruby/>) for the first time. Presenter Alex Sharp shared a presentation on Test Driven Development (TDD) with [Shoulda](<http://thoughtbot.com/projects/shoulda/>), a set of helpers that sit on top of the Test::Unit framework. You can see a blog post he made on the subject [at his blog](<http://www.alexjsharp.com/2008/10/15/shoulda-painless-unit-testing>).

The group was a good mix of Rails veterans, casual users, and even people who have never used rails. Josh Crews headed the group up and started out with everyone introducing themselves and getting to know each other. Afterwards we had open discussion on topics ranging from Rails skeleton apps like [Bort](<http://github.com/fudgestudios/bort/tree/master>) and [Suspenders](<http://giantrobots.thoughtbot.com/2008/10/21/suspenders>), to ORM implementations in Ruby, to Gems and Plugins like [AuthLogic](<http://github.com/binarylogic/authlogic/tree/master>).

Alex Sharp gave a 30-45 minute demo of Shoulda, where he created a new project -- the standard blogPost app -- and tested it at each step of the way. He explained the principals and benefits of TDD, showed us how to run tests, how to write unit and functional tests, and explained where Shoulda excels over other testing Frameworks/Gems/Plugins.

Following the presentation we had more open discussion and CentreSource invited the attendees to a night of video gaming and junk food eating. All in all, the meetup and the night were a success, and Alex Sharp (aka drumwerzel) destroyed me at [Urban Terror](<http://www.urbanterror.net/news.php>). [Join us](<http://www.meetup.com/nashvilleruby/>) next time, get your learn on, and maybe even test your first person shooter skills against the gaming force that is CentreSource.
