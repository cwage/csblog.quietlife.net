A few weeks ago, AOL received a [lot of flak](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/30/aol-verisign-truste-direct-revenue/>) for Netscape's new ["trust rating" system](<http://browser.netscape.com/ns8/security/default.jsp>), its system for ranking the danger posed by a particular website. The problem is with some of the [partners it "trusts" implicitly](<http://browser.netscape.com/ns8/security/trust_partners.jsp>). which includes such luminaries as: VeriSign, TRUSTe, ParetoLogic. VeriSign openly acknowledges it does nothing to actually check the security of the sites it certifies, and Spyware Warrior provides just a smattering of domains that are "certified":

> abetterinternet.com  
>  cursormania.com  
>  direct-revenue.com (creators of Aurora and nail.exe)  
>  ebates.com  
>  ezula.com  
>  funbuddyicons.com  
>  funwebproducts.com  
>  hotbar.com  
>  iwon.com  
>  mypctuneup.com  
>  myway.com  
>  mywebsearch.com  
>  sex.com  
>  smileycentral.com  
>  weatherbug.com  
>  webhancer.com  
>  whenu.com 

Well, it's gotten worse. Direct Revenue, a notorious spyware/adware company, has launched a new "product" called [Aurora](<http://www.direct-revenue.com/news6.php>), which is out and out spyware. Over at Spywarewarrior, folks are [hoppin' mad](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/06/06/over-30000-search-engines-hits-for-nailexe-aurora/>) and going as far as to start talking class-action lawsuits:

> For aurora, I got a total of 17,896 search engine hits. These numbers include search strings like "remove nail.exe and aurora pop-ups. If you read the blog comments, [people](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2476>) are [angry](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2478>) as [hell](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2547>). People are talking [class action](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2585>) [lawsuits](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2637>). One person [wrote to his congresswoman](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2549>). Another person [called CEO Joahua Abrams](<http://netrn.net/spywareblog/archives/2005/05/10/got-aurora-nailexe/#comment-2634>) on the phone. 

Let's hope they aim one of those lawsuits at AOL for this abomination. This is one of the essential problems with any system of "trust" that relies on a centralized infrastructure of largely corporate authorities, which we have [railed against as a racket](<http://blog.centresource.com/2005/05/17/swirbos-ssl-certificates/>) in the past. Can we really be expected to trust AOL to certify adware/spyware free websites when AOL's business model is largely refocused entirely on advertising?
