Amazon just released a new program called "[Mechanical Turk](<http://www.mturk.com/>)", which allows people to earn money by performing small tasks that are easier for a person to do than program a computer to do. One of their major focuses is on photography, which has long troubled programmers-going far beyond simple color/shape identification, but also relating to subjective questions like "which is a better example of..." or "what is easier to identify as...". Users will get paid a few pennies for each HIT (human intellegence task) that they complete, which, although not much, can add up with a few hours of clicking.

The name "Mechanical Turk" comes from a [19th century hoax](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mechanical_Turk>) of an automated chess player that was really operated by a midget who was hidden through an optical illusion. With this release by Amazon, it reminds programmers to ask if some of the tasks that we spend hours (or years) developing could be completed easier, more efficiently, or just plain _better_ by a human. If so, we may need to start making use of more artificial artifical intelligence like this.
