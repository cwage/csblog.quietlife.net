I always knew one day robots would take over my job.

And no offense to [CS Maxximus](<http://blog.centresource.com/2010/10/22/guest-post-cs-maxximus-tells-the-story-of-the-2010-centresource-barcamp-sponsorship-campaign/>), of course, but I've always wanted to believe that the creative arts have a long life ahead of them because they're just that: creative (read: non-robotic.)

But, alas, the beginning of the end may be here for me. I introduce to you the [Sloganizer.](<http://www.sloganizer.net/en/>)

Yes, it's exactly what you think it is: an automated slogan creator. Simply enter a word in the search box and boom: the perfect slogan.

Good thing they haven't taught it how to make puns or I'll be out on the streets by Sunday.

As an example, I typed in 'centresource' and here are their ideas for us:

«**Centresource - a class of it's own** »

«**Centresource will be for you what you want it to be.** »

«**There is no life without Centresource.** »

«**Centresource, i want it all.** »

«**Centresource, it's as simple as that!** »

«**Centresource - Yabba Dabba Duh!** »

Ok. Looks like my job may still be safe after all.

Pretty funny concept though and hey, copywriters, if you're in a real bind and need some inspiration, check it out (or don't.)
