If you were born in a certain era you could not escape the pop chart topping bliss of Hall and Oates. But you wouldn't want to escape because they were awesome. This mustachioed super group scored six #1 hits in their heyday and in the process, influenced the way I approach work and company culture.

The first lesson learned came straight out of "Rich Girl", which chronicles the lack of character development for someone who has never had to work hard. Here, Hall and Oates channel something of Ghandi when they echo that wealth without work is deadly. Nothing comes easy and learning how to put your nose to the grindstone and actually accomplish something meaningful takes hard work. Thank you, Rich Girl.

Next up is "I Can't Go for That (No Can Do)". Here we find Hall coming to grips with his own limits. He can't do everything and neither can we. It is important to know where you excel and where you should draw the line. When you focus on maximizing your strengths you'll find yourself getting far more done (and have a better time doing it). Learn to say 'no can do'.

Finally there is "Say It Isn't So", a classic 80s song thick with synthesizers that highlights the timeless truth that clear communication is the key to any relationship. This is valid for romance, but equally as valid in business. Openly discussing expectations lays the groundwork for success. Once again, Hall and Oates nailed it.

Next time you're looking for inspiration to take your work game to the next level, look no further than the timeless genius of [Hall and Oates](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hall_%26_Oates>).
