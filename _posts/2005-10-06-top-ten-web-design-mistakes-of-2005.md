Jakob Nielsen, the "Godfather" of website usability, has produced [his latest list](<https://web.archive.org/web/20051013063651/http://www.useit.com/alertbox/designmistakes.html>) of web site no-no's. Nielsen, founder of [useit.com](<https://web.archive.org/web/20051004093216/http://www.useit.com/>), has long been considered the guru of usability -- how a easy websites (and other user interfaces) are to use. For your average website, a high level of usability is very important, as hard-to-use websites will scare people away faster than you can say "welcome". However, for custom web applications, like the ones that CentreSource builds, high levels of usability are of the utmost importance as we aim to make our applications intuitive, simple, and error free. This allows our clients to spend their time _using_ the application, and not learning it or troubleshooting it.

Check out his site -- any article will be well worth the 5 minutes spent reading it.
