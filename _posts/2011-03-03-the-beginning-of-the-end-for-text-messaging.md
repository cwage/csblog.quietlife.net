Chances are you think I am crazy just by reading the headline.

But if you think about it, is it really unrealistic to believe that a company like Facebook could replace the need for text messaging?

As quick as Facebook has grown, adding features people check everyday, every hour, every minute. Think about it. And with the announcement that **[Facebook is acquiring group text messaging start-up Beluga](<http://www.readwriteweb.com/archives/a_sneak_peek_into_the_future_of_beluga_going_beyon.php>) ,** a user could eliminate the need for text messaging. The app itself will obviously not look the same after Facebook gets a hold of it, but the platform certainly offers rich features.  
[Beluga](<http://belugapods.com/>) is a messaging application for iphone and Android for staying in touch with contacts on the go. Users can send messages via Beluga's application to numbers, emails, and groups. You create a "pod" (Beluga's name for a group) of people and each time one of you sends a message, everyone else gets it. Your message can send pictures, locations and text. The best thing about Beluga is that it does not limit its reach to only smart phones. The app can operate for traditional phones as well by sending them SMS instead of in-app content

In a nutshell this acquisition reveals Facebook's motivation to own more of our messaging and communication. There is endless potential here for Facebook. Video messaging, emails, location based messaging etc. Think about how Fan Pages or even the everyday user could use these messaging features. While it maybe unlikely for Facebook to replace the need for text messaging right now, it could be on the horizon. So how much do I pay for text messaging?
