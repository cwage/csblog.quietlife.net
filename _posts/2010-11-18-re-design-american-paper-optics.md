When American Paper Optics came to us looking for help, we knew a few things up front: they were innovators of technology, notable leaders in their industry and the world's largest producer of 3D glasses.

They also had a web site that didn't do any of that a justice.  


Enter: Centresource.

Our main objective on this project was to match their site with their excellent reputation, standards and assets. Looking at the redesign through the eyes of their stakeholder, the Centresource design team created a new site that was interactive, fun and inviting. Furthermore, we prioritized the organization of the content into a streamlined narrative that clearly explained the technology involved in their product. 

At the end of the day, our job was to highlight and showcase American Paper Optics as the industry leader that they are. With a company and product as outstanding as theirs, it was not a difficult task.

The lead designer on this project, Jason O'Brien, weighs in on his vision and direction for the re-design:

> _The previous site lacked any sense of company branding... it could have been any random site on the internet.__Every page used a different template and lacked consistency. Even the navigation would change location and wording from section to section. Their goal was to correct these issues and also come up with a visual solution that put them ahead of their competitors._
> 
> _  
> _
> 
> _We used a technique called parallax to embody the concept of "3D" on the homepage. As you move your mouse around, layers of images will move separately and give the user a sense of depth. The client loved this unique way to convey who they were as a company._
