When Google first released its [Maps site](<http://maps.google.com/>), I knew that great things were in our future. The layout is simplistic, but that amazing map simply captivates so many people that view it.

A recent article in the NYT confirms that Google's new service (and its open API) have great potential. Paul Rademacher, a 30 year old software developer for Dreamworks studio, created a fantastic site: [HousingMaps.com](<http://housingmaps.com/>)

The site combines the data feed from Craigslist (a popular listing site) with the Google Maps interface to create a unique house hunting experience.

Rather than search by standard criteria, people can quickly see if a house is near an interstate, or a particular cross street - by simply looking at the visually stunning Google Map!
