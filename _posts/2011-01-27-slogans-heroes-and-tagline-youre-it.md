I love a good slogan.

I also love a good tag line.

But, if you ask most people, the distinctions between the two are blurry.

And a lot of the time, the two are used interchangeably.

While both are an integral part of a company's identity and while they also share many similarities -attention-getting, memorable, brand-centric, descriptive-they arguably each play a different role in a company's branding.

So I asked myself, does anyone care?

Or rather, do the differences between the two matter?

So I polled some of the kids at centresource and I took a look at the 'ol google machine. And here's what I found.

**The Nuts and Bolts**

  * The word slogan comes from a Gaelic word (_sluagh-ghairm_) meaning "battle cry." It is associated with specific campaigns for your company/product/service. Slogans change all the time depending on the campaign and have lots of room for creativity.
  * The tagline, however, is what stays with your company wherever you go. Otherwise put: if a slogan is for a battle, a tag line is for the war.



**For Example.**

Let's look at a few examples of both:

  * A famous slogan for Wendy's is, "Where's the beef?" while their tagline is " _You Know When It's Real._ "
  * Ford's historic tagline is, " _Designed for Living. Engineered to Last_." But a quick visit to their website reveals a slogan for their new 2011 cars, "Not all technology fits in your pocket."
  * Verizon Wireless had a famous campaign with the memorable slogan, "Can you hear me now?" but their tagline is " _We Never Stop Working for You"_



**A Lot of Questions.**

The deeper I got into this topic, the more confused I became. Several brands have their tagline underneath their logo, some don't have it anywhere on their site, and others link directly to their current campaign and slogan.

Offline initiatives were even more misleading. Asking co-workers the difference between a slogan and a tagline gave me a plethora of different responses and asking people to tell me a certain brand's slogan or tagline left me with even less clarity than before.

To me, it appears that the thing that matters most to a consumer is **brand recognition and loyalty**. So can both your tagline and slogan accomplish this? Should your tagline be a catchy, memorable explanation of your value proposition while your slogans vary depending on the campaign, product or promotion?

> What qualities make a good tagline versus a good slogan?

Should you anchor one tagline forever and have slogans only for different campaigns?

Or does your tagline change as your company/product/services evolve? Like Coca Cola (It's the Real Thing, You Know When It's Real, Always Coca-Cola) or Visa (from 'It's Everywhere You Wanna Be' to 'Life Takes Visa')and are you ok with your taglines and slogans becoming interchangeable in the mind of the consumer like Taco Bell (Make a run for the border, Yo Quiero Taco Bell, Think Outside the Bun)?

> If the most important thing is brand recognition and brand loyalty, than you need your tagline and slogans to elicit an emotional experience that triggers your consumer's memory and compels them to buy what you are selling....over and over again.

So if your tagline does that, great. And if it takes you 19 slogans to accomplish that, so be it.
