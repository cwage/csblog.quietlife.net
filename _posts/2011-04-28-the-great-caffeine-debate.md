It's 8 am. Across the US millions of people on the way to work are thinking of only one thing - caffeine. How quickly can I get this into my system? Which delivery method should I choose? Do I have to produce anything myself or do I have enough change in the cup holder to swing by Starbucks?

We are no different at CS, but recently we've begun breaking into different caffeine sub-groups; the coffee snobs, the tea drinking hippies, and the energy drink fiends.

The coffee snobs won't touch anything made in a semi-mass market fashion. They love slow drip, french-press, micro-roasted coffee. Can they taste the difference? They swear they can. I'm doubtful it actually affects the caffeine intake in anyway, but for them, it's about taste. Cost is also irrelevant to this high class coffee connoisseur. Prepare to be yelled at if you mention 'Folgers.'

The tea drinking hippies are all about anti-oxidants. I'm not sure what that is but they swear it's more important then the caffeine itself. I don't trust this. Tea drinking comes with it's own set of accoutrements and brewing rules that tea drinkers follow religiously. The problem is there are too many choices. Who has the time to decide between Rooibos and Earl Grey with White Tips? And don't even talk to me about steeping!

Finally, there is the UFC fighting champion of caffeine drinkers, the energy drink fiends. By all appearances these people have transcended the normal physiological rules about how much caffeine a person can ingest in a given day. These beverages have names like Crank, Monster, ReHab, and my personal favorite, Xyience. Personally, any drink that makes my heart rate hit 160 bpm while sitting at my desk is not an option.

For the rest of us schlubs, do I add the powdered creamer or the splenda first into my Kroger brand knockoff 'medium bold.'?

Tell us, what's your poison and why?
