The final installment of my quick Workamajig review involves that most quintessential business item; cash. Without it, we can't meet payroll or update equipment. We can't look to the future and see what kind of position we should next hire. We can't even keep the lights on. It stands to reason then, that knowing how your company is spending and processing money is a necessity. Yet so many young creative firms have only a ballpark idea of what their bottom line is, and how it got be that way.

The WMJ answer to your finances falls into two categories: Reports and Widgets. The reporting section of WMJ comes with a slew of out of the box reports that will give you all kinds of info. You can run detailed analysis comparing your budget for the year to your actuals. With a quick click pull up your Balance Sheet or run a client level Profit and Loss report. Sometimes you need to dive deeper or take a different approach. WMJ accounts for this via a 'Custom Reports' feature that will let you build what you need from across different data sets. Getting the speed of this can take some time, but I can honestly say that there are two custom reports I depend on every day. The only critique is that you will occasionally run into a roadblock due to the way certain data sets don't share information.

Often we don't have the time during the course of a busy day to shift through a ton of reports loaded with data. This is where the Widget shine. WMJ allows you to pull into your Dashboard any group of Widgets composed of financial graphs. There is the Metrics Monitor which gives you a rundown on seventeen benchmarks and where you currently stand. There is the Cash on Hand graph showing you cash flow for the last six months. There is even a Chargeability Summary which gives you a birds-eye view of how much billable project work is being done, and of that how much you are actually collecting. This is all invaluable data that you can pull up in seconds.

Without a clear idea of your companies metrics, making educated decisions that increase profitability is nearly impossible.
