[![](/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/kraft-macaroni-and-cheese-profile-200x300.jpg)](</wp-content/uploads/2011/03/kraft-macaroni-and-cheese-profile.jpg> "kraft-macaroni-and-cheese-profile")When I saw people tweeting about Mac n Cheese 2 weeks ago, I didn't really think much of it.

I have hungry friends, after all. And we live in the land of comfort food.

But when I saw the hashtag [#MacJinx](<http://twitter.com/#!/search?q=%23MacJinx>), my twitter interest (twinterest?) was piqued.

After some minor detective work (ok, just clicking on the hashtage and then on [@kraftmacncheese profile](<http://twitter.com/#!/kraftmacncheese>)) I discovered a really fun, easy interactive contest being put on my the household boxed dinner.

The rules were simple: 1. Tweet something about Macaroni and Cheese 2. Cross your fingers that you tweeted at the same time as someone else and that the minds behind the twitter handle notice 3. Receive "MacJinx" notification tweet from @kraftmacncheese with instructions on how to register for prizes 4. Beat out your fellow macjinx tweeter to sign up on the site. 5. Receive a box in the mail with tons of free goodies.

So I tweeted. And waited. And won.

And this came in the mail last week:

[![](/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/photo-5-300x225.jpg)](</wp-content/uploads/2011/03/photo-5.jpg> "cheesy prizes")

Winning never tasted so cheesy.

As if that weren't enough, they are now pulling an Old Spice and [taking tweets from their followers and turning them into national TV Ads](<http://mashable.com/2011/03/29/kraft-mac-cheese-ad-conan/>):

[![](/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Picture-18-300x48.png)](</wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Picture-18.png> "Picture 18")

Not sure how they are measuring the success of these recent initiatives, but from a buzz perspective? So far, so good, Kraft.
