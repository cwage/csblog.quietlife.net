There's a very lauditory article over on ONLamp about the value of the combination of [AJAX and Ruby on Rails](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050613014411/http://www.onlamp.com/pub/a/onlamp/2005/06/09/rails_ajax.html>). Specifically, it mentions the power in using XMLHttpRequest to do some pretty impressive client-to-server communications without reloading the entire page using HTTP POST or something similar.

The page includes a [demo](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050613005535/http://blog.curthibbs.us/articles/2005/01/01/ajax-on-rails>) of this functionality. What's troubling to me is that, well, it doesn't work in [Opera](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050613013415/http://www.opera.com/>). Now, whatever you may think of Opera (first person to say "get a real browser" gets a smack to the forehead), I think we can all agree that a web technology that is not widely supported is dangerous. And, as ever, I am wary of building a web interface that relies on things like javascript and this newer stuff, because in my experience it's never worth the pain in trying to troubleshoot an instance where it's just not supported (or is supported but broken).

So, I thought I'd just lob this out there for purposes of discussion: does anyone know how widely supported things like XMLHttpRequest are in various browsers? Is AJAX going to become a gold standard in the next iteration of defacto standard web development or is it just another cutting-edge toy?
