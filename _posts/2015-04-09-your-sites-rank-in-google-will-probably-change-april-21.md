![Google](https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/04/Google_G.svg_-297x300.png)Google recently did you a HUGE favor, and you may have missed it. Long notorious for rolling out changes to its search algorithm unannounced, Google actually gave us all a big heads-up on an upcoming change to the algorithm which will have a major impact on search results on mobile devices.

For years now, Google has said that [responsive design is its preferred method](<http://googlewebmastercentral.blogspot.com/2012/06/recommendations-for-building-smartphone.html>) for smartphone-optimized websites. Late last year, Google added a "[Mobile-friendly" label to sites](<http://googlewebmastercentral.blogspot.com/2014/11/helping-users-find-mobile-friendly-pages.html>) that were optimized for viewing on a smartphone. Now, [Google has announced](<http://googlewebmastercentral.blogspot.com/2015/02/finding-more-mobile-friendly-search.html>) that it is rolling out an algorithm change on April 21 that will use mobile friendliness to determine where your site ranks in search results. In Google's own words, this will have a " _significant impact in our search results_."

So, with April 21 just around the corner,**how can you prepare?**

  * First, test the mobile-readiness of your site using Google's [Mobile-Friendly Test](<https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/mobile-friendly/>).
  * Next, if you use Webmaster Tools, check your site's [Mobile Usability Report](<https://www.google.com/webmasters/tools/mobile-usability>). This report shows you common usability issues on your mobile site. This can provide the direction needed to fix the issues, ensuring a positive user experience for your mobile users.
  * Finally, check out Google's [Webmaster's Mobile Guide](<https://developers.google.com/webmasters/mobile-sites/>) which provides tons of information about mobile best practices, and how to[ make virtually any CMS-based site mobile friendly](<https://developers.google.com/webmasters/mobile-sites/website-software/>). It also offers advice on [mobile SEO](<https://developers.google.com/webmasters/mobile-sites/mobile-seo/>) best practices.



Don't find yourself blindsided on April 21. Take advantage of this early warning from Google. Check your site and then fix any mobile usability errors you find. Make sure that the change to your search ranking on April 21 is higher up, not off the page!
