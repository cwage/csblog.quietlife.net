Palm OS, the platform that used to be the trend-setter for PDA's looks like it may be on it's last breath as it was [announced today](<http://www.betanews.com/article/Access_Buys_PalmSource_and_Palm_OS/1126279932>) that the Japanese-based Access would buy PalmSource, Inc. for just under $325 million. Of course all of this happened after Palm, Inc. bought the rising Handspring, Inc and then split into PalmSource (the software side) and palmOne (the hardware side, which has now been renamed back to Palm). The split was originally meant to allow for better negotiating power on both the hardware and software side, but instead caused the Palm OS software to lose the close tie with its biggest ally (itself). As such, PalmSource was already looking to develop in Linux, as palmOne was looking towards Windows Mobile.

Time will tell the fate of the former giant, Palm OS.
