The end of summer signals the start of great times in Nashville. Aside from the start of football and hockey season, it also means that [BarCamp Nashville](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/>) is just around the corner. Entering its fourth year as a mainstay in the interactive community in the middle Tennessee area, this year's event returns Saturday, October 16 to [Cadillac Ranch on Broadway](<http://maps.google.com/maps/place?cid=8248122209257652856>).

The conference revolves around sessions created by some of the best and brightest in Nashville, covering topics ranging from how to build your brand using social media to how to implement the latest version of an API. Check out the [list of sessions](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/sessions/all>) and you are bound to find something that piques your interest.

_If you have never been to BarCamp:_

  * [Register on the site](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/user/register>).
  * Complete your profile (be sure to mark "Yes" to the "Are you attending ..." question).
  * Come out and see us on at 8 a.m. on October 16 at Cadillac Ranch!



_If you attended BarCamp Nashville 2009:_

**Update:** Centresource CEO Nicholas Holland did an interview with the BarCamp organizers about our past involvement. See below!

> ### Sponsors Talk BarCamp Benefits
> 
> It's very simple... [BarCamp Nashville](<http://e2ma.net/go/8645356893/3107010/98684030/12277/b64/aHR0cDovL3d3dy5iYXJjYW1wbmFzaHZpbGxlLm9yZy9iY24xMC8/dXRtX3NvdXJjZT1CQ04xMCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPUVtYWlsJnV0bV9jb250ZW50PUJDTjEwbGluayZ1dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249QkNOMTB3azRTZXNzaW9uQ3JlYXRpb25SZU9wZW4=> "BarCamp Nashville") could not be what it is without the ongoing sponsorship support from Nashville's tech-inspired community. At the same time, those businesses and organizations frequently find that the fruits of their support comes back to them in unexpected ways.
> 
> One of our legacy sponsors is Centresource Interactive Agency, the Germantown-based web design, development, rich media and interactive strategy firm. Centresource has been invaluable in supporting the BCN mission and we recently caught up with Nicholas Holland, President & CEO, to ask why they keep coming back for more
> 
> **BCN:** How has sponsoring BarCamp Nashville helped your business?  
>  **Holland:** BarCamp has been good for the overall community in bringing excitement to the New Media and Interactive industries. It's been an excellent source for new relationships -- many of which have turned into new employees.
> 
> The people we've met at BarCamp go beyond just business relationships, they are people we actually enjoy spending time with. Additionally, BarCamp has been a great opportunity for our team to give back to the Nashville interactive community by speaking on topics that they're passionate about.
> 
> **BCN:** Do you think sponsoring BarCamp Nashville has helped position your brand in a favorable way among the tech community and if so, how?  
>  **Holland:** To some degree I think the tech community certainly appreciates the sponsors of BarCamp. The true value for us, however, has been in being able to back our claim that we support the community.
> 
> In a town like Nashville, we have to work together to raise awareness of what we do, so it is key to be able to tell prospects that we are a leader in our community. Sponsoring BarCamp backs that up.
> 
> **BCN:** Anything else notable that keeps you involved year after year?  
>  **Holland:** The thing that is both impressive and unique about BarCamp Nashville is that it's a free conference put on by volunteers, yet it's the largest tech conference in Nashville. This speaks volumes about the quality and tone of the event and the culture of our community.
