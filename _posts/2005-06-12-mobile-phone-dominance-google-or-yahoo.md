I was reading [Business 2.0 Blog](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050613234100/http://business2.blogs.com/business2blog/>) and [I found this article about Yahoo & Google](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050613234915/http://business2.blogs.com/business2blog/2005/06/yahoo_vs_google.html>) gearing up for a battle over the mobile phone landscape. It was interesting, because I've been using the bleeding edge of mobile phone technology for the past 2 years. I was the guy that would actually log into AOL on his Sprint Phone - and then try to have chat conversations while driving the car.

I've used both, and the article is right on... Yahoo makes it much easier to use their service than Google. First off, I rarely want to do a simple search on my phone. I use it more for things like the movies, directions, weather, and news. In these areas, the portal approach crushes the search approach.

In fact, the phone is so slow and unwieldy - the Google approach is actually annoying. I can't afford to have ANY extra time wasted given how slow the phone net use is in the first place.

If I had to complain about the Yahoo service, its that they've started including pictures with their news articles... it takes forever to load the articles. This is one area where options to turn off images would be awesome. Also, the first service to accept voice integration will get a major thumbs up in my book.
