We recently had the pleasure of working with [HookUP Media](<https://web.archive.org/web/20100310113805/http://www.hookup-media.com/>) to help produce the Web site for "[March for California's Future](<https://web.archive.org/web/20100205194521/http://www.fight4cafuture.com/>)," an online companion to an event going on right now in the Golden State. Organizers say that the 250 mile march, sponsored by the [California Federation of Teachers](<https://web.archive.org/web/20100117162203/http://www.cft.org/>) and its diverse allies from labor, the faith community, and other civic supporters, is designed to draw attention to the underlying causes of the state's woes as well as gather signatures for a majority budget initiative.

Visitors are invited to take action by signing the online petition, donating to the cause, and even joining the marchers as they travel from city to city across the state. A core team of marchers staff the roaming "Blogmobile," and a [Google Latitude](<https://web.archive.org/web/20090719233052/http://www.google.com/latitude>) map is updated throughout the day on a mobile phone.

Check out the site at <https://web.archive.org/web/20100309/http://www.fight4cafuture.com/>.
