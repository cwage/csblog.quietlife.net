It's been an interesting year at CentreSource. Our focus this year has been mostly internal -- we've been honing our internal processes and working closely with our now 20-person team to make our day-to-day as efficient as possible. We're also learning from each other: we spend every day in peer meetings as a means to cross train each other and make sure that all the knowledge we're gathering from every new client experience is shared with the team. In effect, we continue to raise our own bar every day -- something that's easy to say but harder to do, because the more the bar raises, the more we expect from ourselves.

It's encouraging, then, to see some recognition for our hard work. We received two great pieces of press this month worth sharing:

First, we were selected again this year[ for the Nashville Chamber of Commerce's Future 50 award](<http://www.tennessean.com/article/20090713/BUSINESS01/907130319/1003/BUSINESS/Nashville+chamber+names+Future+50+winners>). This is our second year of being apart of this great group, and we're honored to be placed among 49 other fast-growing Nashville companies. We're also thrilled to see so many tech and interactive-based companies on the list -- further proof that Nashville's tech community is thriving.

Secondly, [CentreSource was recently featured in the Nashville Business Journal](<http://nashville.bizjournals.com/nashville/stories/2009/07/06/smallb2.html>). The interview with Evan Owens, our Director of Business Development, tells the story of our growth over the last six years, and the challenge in balancing small clients and big clients as a growing firm. It's a good read, and Evan tells our story well with lots of transparency into some of the obstacles we've faced.
