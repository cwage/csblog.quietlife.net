We have some special surprises in store for [BarCamp Nashville](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/>) that you will not want to miss! On top of that, two of our team members will be giving presentations throughout the day that promise to be fun and entertaining.

[Confessions of a Blackberry, iOS, Android User](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/session/confessions-blackberry-ios-android-user>)

> _Presenter:[Nicholas Holland](<http://blog.centresource.com/author/nholland>)_  
>  Remember what your parents taught you? Never talk about Religion or Politics because it always leads to an argument. Well, add Mobile Phones to that list. The iPhone has become one of the most divisive products in the history of man & its fanatical culture (both love/hate) is spilling over into other mobile platforms.

[Information Overload - Why and how you should filter out noise to unearth relevant and meaningful information](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/session/information-overload>)

> _Panel: Nate Baker and[Chip Hayner](<http://blog.centresource.com/author/chayner>)_  
>  We'll discuss theories on how to deal with information overload. This isn't a talk just about how to filter email and google reader. It will have practical applications, but it will start by explaining why it's vitally important that we understand what role information plays in our life. Hint: Information is not supposed to be distracting or give us a feeling we're drowning. Here, we have a towel.

[Marketing Douchebags; SEO, Social, and Beyond](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/session/panel-marketing-douchebags-seo-social-and-beyond>)

> _Panel: John Ellis, Joey Strawn, Scott Gordon and[Nicholas Holland](<http://blog.centresource.com/author/nholland>)_  
>  This panel discussion will bring together Nashville area minds to discuss, what else, douchebags. From an SEO and Social Media mindset, is it true nice guys finish last? Can you be nice, open and share with others, and still make a profit? 

The event is 7:30 a.m. - 4:30 p.m. this Saturday, October 16 in downtown Nashville at [Cadillac Ranch](<http://maps.google.com/maps/place?cid=8248122209257652856&gl=us>). Even if you can only make it for part of the day, it will be well worth it. They have a [full day of sessions](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/sessions/all>) planned out. Did we mention that it is _free_?

Check out this [Survival Guide](<http://www.barcampnashville.org/bcn10/barcamp-nashville-2010-survival-guide>) that the BarCamp Crew put together to help you get the most out of the event. We hope to see you there!
