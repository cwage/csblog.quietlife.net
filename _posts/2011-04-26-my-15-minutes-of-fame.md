It all started at 10:30 at night on Friday, April 8th. My super talented friends [Katie Moore](<http://thisnotthis.tumblr.com/>) and [Austin Gros ](<http://austingros.com/>)and I wanted something fun to do so we channeled our inner 13 year old selves, set up a video camera and entered the CNN iReport Royal Wedding contest. The rules were you must create a 90 second video stating why CNN should choose you to report on the royal wedding in London.

CNN wished to see something creative and unique. Here is our video attempt as royal wedding correspondents: WARNING: This video contains shirtless pictures of Prince Harry.

[Send us to the Royal Wedding! Please?!](<http://ireport.cnn.com/docs/DOC-588667>)

We loaded our video on Sunday night and by the end of Monday evening, we had almost 2,000 views. The video spread like hot cakes and before we knew it our Twitter and Facebook feeds were overwhelmed with our royal wedding video. I always knew social media could be really helpful in these types of situations, but I had never actually experienced it.

On Tuesday, the news of two crazy girls trying to go to the royal wedding had spread even further and our local news channel 5 came to my home to interview us. Click here to watch our local spotlight:

[Two Local Women Compete for Chance at Royal Wedding](<http://www.newschannel5.com/story/14434919/local-women-compete-for-chance-at-royal-wedding>)

Wednesday morning came and CNN had contacted Katie and I to see if we had our passports. We took this as a pretty clear bet that we'd be attending the royal wedding and that we needed to get new dresses ASAP. We continued to push the video in hopes that we'd become the number 1 viewed video on the CNN iReport page. We accomplished that goal thanks to twitter and facebook. People across the pond such as NET Magazine even believed in our love for the British and supported us.

The following Monday was D-Day. We got a heartfelt call by a CNN employee that sadly, we did not win. He stated they deliberated over our video for hours, loved it, but had chosen someone else. After that let down, we decided to lose gracefully by replying to CNN in the only way we knew how..a video montage.

[These Colors Don't Run](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2G3eiQsze6I>)

Even though we didn't win a trip to England to report on the royal wedding, we had a lot of fun trying and really appreciate all our friends and family who helped spread the news throughout the land of the internet!

Beth
