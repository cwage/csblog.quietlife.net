I know I'm a little late to the game and our CEO Nick Holland might go all ["Executive" ](<http://goo.gl/5tQU0>)on me for writing this before him, but I have something that might change your life (that is, if you're a chronically unorganized person like myself). 

Evernote. 

Oh Evernote. I kept hearing about you like that restaurant on the other side of town that everyone loved but I had never been to, which meant of course that I would have to TRAVEL to the other side of town which is terribly inconvenient (and the Belmont hipsters make me nervous). 

I digress.

If you're like me you spend a lot of time researching on the internet looking for articles or flipping through brilliant work from designers around the world. You often take screenshots or bookmark things that are particularly interesting to you such as, "Maybe I want to try out this CSS3 modal window later", or "this vegetarian burrito recipe might change my life" or "maybe this tiny cute kitten video I found during my lunch break *ahem* needs to be catalogued for further review."

My normal workflow would be to grab a screenshot, forget to name it with a remotely appropriate file name, throw it in a nondescript folder named ORGANIZE THIS OR FACE IMPENDING DOOM, AWESOME, WORK STUFF, FUN STUFF, etc and pray for sweet mercy from our heavenly father that I miraculously find it again when I need it.

Now with Evernote I have everything organized into "Notebooks" for all of my interests, including: CSS3, HTML5, Typography, Web Design, Recipes, Vacation Ideas, etc. Each Notebook is filled with different notes about that topic, complete with pictures, HTML page clippings, my personal notes about it, and tags to help me further categorize and more importantly SEARCH for that article or design later. 

So do yourself a favor and learn more about [Evernote](<http://www.evernote.com/>). 

You owe it to your sanity. You owe it to yourself. And now you kind of owe it to me.
