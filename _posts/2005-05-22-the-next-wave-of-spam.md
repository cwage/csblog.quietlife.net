date: 2005-05-22

Larry Seltzer[ commented](<http://blog.ziffdavis.com/seltzer/archive/2005/05/09/6892.aspx>) about a company switching from standard emails to a combination of GIF and PDF's to avoid False Positives.

If a legitimate company is making this change (and a company that sends out information about Spam nonetheless) is doing this, then what is to stop spammers from doing the same?

We already see some of our Swirbo users marking false negatives that have what appears to be a short blurb of 'normal' text, followed by a large animated GIF about Viagra or Cialis! The content filters in Swirbo can not analyze the image, so the offending content is not noticed.

This puts a great deal of pressure on the rest of our filters and techniques since the content filters are basically useless in this scenario.

Does this signify the next wave of spam? Millions of emails being sent that have innocuous text, followed by an image or PDF file with the offending spam. If this is the case, the next wave of Spam services better have image analyzers and PDF content readers.

I agree with Larry - the SMTP protocol is flawed and thus gives us a broken framework to deal with.
