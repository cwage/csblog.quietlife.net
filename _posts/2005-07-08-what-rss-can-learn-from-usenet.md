Chris has a [great post](<https://web.archive.org/web/20060925231409/http://utcc.utoronto.ca/~cks/space/blog/tech/RSSAndUsenet>) summarizing what RSS aggregator authors should learn from features that have existed for years in clients like NNTP readers. These are thoughts I have had as well. There's never been an RSS aggregator I have particularly liked. My excuse for not writing an alternative is that I am not much of a programmer.

Bloglines is about as good as it's gotten for me. If I could get bloglines on a local server I control with a button I could push to update all the feeds, I might be happy .. for a while
