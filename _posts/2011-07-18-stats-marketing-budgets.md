Surprising Stats: According to Nielsen, retailers still spend an estimated 60-70 percent of their marketing budget on printed ad circulars.

Despite a drop in response rates from 28 percent in 2005 down to 20 percent in 2010, printed circulars are still taking 60-70 percent of marketing budgets. Pretty staggering when you stop and think about it. Nielsen's "[Browse All About it! The Evolution of the Circular](<http://blog.nielsen.com/nielsenwire/consumer/browse-all-about-it-the-evolution-of-the-circular/>)" article this month has quite a few nuggets of interesting statistics. While print certainly has its loyal followers, its clear that is has lost some effectiveness.

This article does a great job of showcasing how different advertising mediums must work together. Integrated marketing campaigns that leverage multiple mediums time and time again prove in the best results. The fact that only 20 percent of store shoppers are visiting these retailers site, demonstrates there is a lack of strategy. One medium must feed the other for the best results. While print still has its great reach, the better ROI can be found in digital.

What do you think? How does your marketing budget compare?
