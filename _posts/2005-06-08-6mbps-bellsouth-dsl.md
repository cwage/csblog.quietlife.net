Looks like Bellsouth has finally [announced some detail](<http://biz.yahoo.com/prnews/050606/clm066.html?.v=9>) on its broadband plans:

> CHICAGO, June 6 /PRNewswire/ -- BellSouth (NYSE: BLS - News) announced plans today to launch a new high-speed broadband service in the fourth quarter of 2005. The company's new service will deliver residential and business customers downstream connection speeds of up to 6Mbps.
> 
> ...
> 
> BellSouth® FastAccess® DSL Xtreme 6.0 will provide residential customers with a fourth option when selecting their FastAccess DSL service. BellSouth's tiered approach to broadband already includes the following speeds:
> 
> \- FastAccess DSL Xtreme, which allows customers to download, surf and  
>  game online at speeds up to 3Mbps downstream X 384Kbps upstream.  
>  \- FastAccess DSL Ultra, ideal for downloading large files, enjoying  
>  streaming video and online games at speeds up to 1.5Mbps downstream X  
>  256Kbps upstream.  
>  \- FastAccess DSL Lite, perfect for e-mailing, Web surfing and online  
>  banking without the delays and hassles of dial-up, at speeds up to  
>  256Kbps downstream X 128Kbps upstream. 

The primary beef with this announcement by the folks at broadbandreports appears to be that they are adding yet another tier, rather than just upgrading all their existing offerings. I'd have to agree. Bellsouth should be looking to simplify and improve rather than adding yet another layer of "advanced" service.
