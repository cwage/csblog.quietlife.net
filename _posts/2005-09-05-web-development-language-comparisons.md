I recently stumbled upon an [excellent email thread](<http://macnoland.blogspot.com/2005/08/javanet-vs-phprubyperlpython.html>) posted by Mac Noland about the importance of choosing a development language in a project. From my perspective, it spoke directly to the question of which development language is best for which project.

From the arguments outlined in this post, I agree with both commentators. The design can be language agnostic & executed effectively in almost any language.

However, our experience in web programming supports 'Brock' in that development will be less expensive and completed very rapidly in a scripting language.

_Pay close attention to the 2 educational research papers that are referenced in the article._

Also see our other articles on [PHP vs .NET](<http://blog.centresource.com/2005/06/06/php-vs-aspnet-the-battle-rages-on/>) and a [good post by 'StupicEverything'](<http://www.stupideverything.ca/?p=60>) comparing PHP, ASP, and JSP.
