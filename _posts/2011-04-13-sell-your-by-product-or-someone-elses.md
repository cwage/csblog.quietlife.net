![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/Screen-shot-2011-04-13-at-7.38.30-PM-300x257.png)There's a new company called "[StickyGram](<http://stickygram.com/>)". They turn your [Instagrams](<http://instagr.am/>) into fridge magnets. StickyGram will print your favorite images as awesome little magnets for you to play with.

They took an existing idea ("[Instagram](<http://instagr.am/>)") and figured out a way to sell a "by-product" (refrigerator magnets). When I saw this, it reminded me of a chapter in the book, [Rework by Jason Fried of 37 Signals](<http://37signals.com/rework/>) entitled, "Sell your by-product."

_(an excerpt from Rework)_

__ "Henry Ford learned a process for turning wood scraps from the production of Model T's into charcoal briquets. He built a charcoal plant and Ford Charcoal was created (later renamed Kingsford Charcoal). Today Kingsford is still the leading manufacturer of charcoal in America."

__ What is a "by-product"? [Wikipedia](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/By-product>) explains it like this: "A **by-product** is a secondary or incidental product deriving from a manufacturing process, a chemical reaction or a biochemical pathway, and is not the primary product or service being produced. A by-product can be useful and marketable."

After finding [StickyGram](<http://stickygram.com/>), I got to thinking about other companies out there that are by-products of another company. The first one that came to mind was [ModerNash](<http://www.modernash.com/>). Their tagline is, "We're not IKEA, we just bring it home." They deliver products purchased at the Atlanta IKEA to people in Nashville.

[Atebits](<http://www.atebits.com/>), a _suspiciously_ simple software company, created the once popular Twitter application called, "Tweetie" then "Tweetie 2". Last April, Twitter [acquired the application](<http://techcrunch.com/2010/04/09/twitter-acquires-tweetie/>). The app is now known as, "Twitter for iPhone."

With all of these by-products on the market this makes me wonder, do I have a by-product to sell?

Chances are you have a by-product to sell too. Figure out what it is and get it out there.
