Several people have asked to have an extra copy of it, so here it is for your reference.

### 8 Ways To Optimize Your Mac's Performance

  1. **Clean Out Startup Items**
     * Apple Menu > System Preferences > Accounts > Login Items
  2. **Turn Off Universal Access, Bluetooth, Speech Recognition, and Internet Sharing**
     * Also in System Preferences - this will save you some valuable RAM and processor resources.
  3. **Clean Out "Other" System Preferences**
     * How many items are down there? Do you know what they do? Do you use what they do? Turn them off!
  4. **Get Rid of Languages and Translations you won't use**
     * Use a utility like "Monolingual" to get rid of Catalan, Farsi, and Klingon.
  5. **Cool Off**
     * Use utilities like "Fan Control" to keep your computer running cooler (though it does shorten battery life a little bit).
  6. **Evaluate Your Widgets**
     * It takes RAM and Processor Power to keep these Widgets running, even when Dashboard is hidden.
  7. **Keep An Eye on Activity Monitor**
     * Activity Monitor will tell you about CPU Usage, RAM Requirements, Virtual Memory Usage, etc.
  8. **Clean Off Your Hard Drive**
     * Sometimes a little more free space is all you need to be a peak performance again!
