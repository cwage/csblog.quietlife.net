In order for an Operations department to move willingly into a ROWE environment, the R must be clearly understood. This gets even trickier at an agency where everyone does something  _slightly_ different. Should a designer be measured in the same way as a developer? What about a strategist tasked with understanding the business needs of our clients? How do you apply equal standards across the board? Carefully. 

The answer for CS really started to coalesce over a year ago. The one consistent item across all pieces of our firm is the billable hour. Why not use this as our baseline standard for results? Certainly there would be other, more subjective items that would go into our ROWE evaluations, but ultimately, it all starts with the billable hour. 

So, every Monday morning we generate a report that compares billable hours tracked vs. anticipated hours for the previous week. We run this on a per employee basis. It's pretty straight forward. Everyone on our team knows what they're shooting for, and they work hard to get it. This lets us, as a firm, have the flexibility to incorporate ROWE without feeling like we're flying blindly. Next post we'll look at how we handle the increased amount of communication that goes into the ROWE system.
