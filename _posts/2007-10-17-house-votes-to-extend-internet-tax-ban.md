date: 2007-10-17
---
> The House overwhelmingly voted Tuesday to extend a soon-to-expire moratorium on Internet access taxes for four years, ignoring pressure to make the ban permanent.
> 
> The bill, which passed in a 405-2 vote, would extend the moratorium through 2011. The existing moratorium is set to expire on Nov. 1.
> 
> "By voting to continue the tax moratorium, the House will help promote innovation, affordable Internet access, and broadband services for American consumers and businesses," said House Speaker Nancy Pelosi, D-Calif.

From [MarketWatch: House votes to extend Internet tax ban](<https://web.archive.org/web/20071018042543/http://www.marketwatch.com/news/story/house-votes-extend-internet-tax/story.aspx?guid=%7BC087FA95-0471-4BE2-8852-4A02A31A2DB9%7D>).

With this Internet tax moratorium, financial pressure will be placed upon states and local governments to look elsewhere for sales tax revenues other than Internet sales. Obviously, online retailers such as eBay and Amazon have a tremendous competitive advantage over local companies that operate with a local storefront. With increasing property taxes, sales tax rates, and regulations, could we see an accelerated demise of the local mom and pop retailer?
