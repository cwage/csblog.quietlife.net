It's [official](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050608015305/http://www.debian.org/News/2005/20050606>): Debian 3.1 "sarge" is [now stable](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050605235036/http://www.debian.org/releases/stable/>).

For those of you wondering (I was), the precedent for EOLing a previous version of Debian as far as maintaining security updates has been around 12 months, but it depends on the security team.

So, this is the timeframe you've got to move servers from woody to sarge. Happy upgrading, and kudos to the Debian team for a very smooth release.
