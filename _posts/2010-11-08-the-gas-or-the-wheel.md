Developers, developers, developers! And I suppose we could also say, Designers, designers, designers! In the world of the Interactive Agency these two groups of people are 'the talent'. The pampered men and women who can demand fresh socks in the green room and refuse to eat unsorted M&Ms. They complete the client facing work, deliver the goods, and reap the recognition when selected by an Awards Committee. But what about the folks lurking in the shadow of these creative giants? Who takes in the invoices, works out the scheduling details, and reports on the bottom line? Operations.

Fortunately I can say that the above description of a class based work environment is not the case at centresource. The Operations Department here is viewed as an integral part of the team. But what does an Operations Department look like in an environment that trends left-brain? Just like any other Ops team, just with a much better feel for comedic timing. At CS we like to build consensus for operational change, instead of delivering our mandates. We look for ways to improve the company and the individual. We deliver serious thoughts with a touch of sarcasm. Perhaps most importantly, we take the long view.

The production staff has their collective foot to the pedal, delivering great projects one after the other. But with that kind of speed, someone needs to have their hands always on the wheel. Operations at CS looks to the Horizon to ensure that our company wide goals are met. With this kind of partnership we've found making minor course corrections a much safer way of doing business.
