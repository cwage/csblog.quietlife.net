So let me preface my first blog entry here by saying that I am certainly not a Microsoft supporter, don't use their products whenever I can get away with it (unless testing for their *amazing* browsers), and I use a Mac on a daily basis.

<http://www.microsoft.com/surface/>

That said, Microsoft does have some cool projects. I'll give them that. I probably won't ever use one of their OSes as a primary environment, but I do have to give them credit for some great ideas. The most recent one is a technology that I have seen before, but they are getting it to the consumer a lot quicker than I had thought. Everybody around technology has seen a demo of this somewhere, but it really looks like MS has put a decent interface on it to make it useful to the standard consumer.

If nothing else, even if the thing throws a BSOD every 5 minutes, at least we can fall back on giving credit to whoever does their marketing. They are doing a decent job :-)
