Bruce Schneier [notes](<http://www.schneier.com/blog/archives/2005/06/seagates_full_d.html>) that Seagate is now offering a [hard drive](<http://www.eweek.com/article2/0,1759,1825740,00.asp>) that offers transparent encryption on the fly. It's low on details, but it looks like it stores a key on the hard-drive itself (bad), but that it's protected by a passphrase (good). This means that the passphrase is ultimately the weakness, obviously. All things considered, it's probably the best way to do this for a mass-marketed drive, but it'd be nice if there were some way to keep the key on an external device of some sort.
