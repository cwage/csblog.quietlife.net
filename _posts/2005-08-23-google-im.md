We've all heard the rumors about Google's new product (*cough*IM*cough*). The natural question was whether or not they would embrace the open standard, XMPP/Jabber.

Check [this out](<https://web.archive.org/web/20071024014438/http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/google-im.png>).

I thought it could be fake, but talk.google.com is definitely real, and definitely listening on port 5222, and speaking XMPP:

> $ telnet talk.google.com 5222  
>  Trying 64.233.167.125...  
>  Connected to talk.google.com.  
>  Escape character is '^]'.  
>  <stream:stream xmlns:stream='http://etherx.jabber.org/streams' xmlns='jabber:client' xml:lang='en-US' to='talk.google.com'>  
>  <stream:stream from="gmail.com" id="E455DB3E" xmlns:stream="http://etherx.jabber.org/streams" xmlns="jabber:client">  
>  Connection closed by foreign host.

**UPDATE:** Okay, I am way behind. Here's [the CNN article](<http://www.cnn.com/2005/TECH/internet/08/23/google.messaging.reut/index.html>).

**UPDATE 2:** Looks like it's up and working. I just logged in using gaim and authenticating with cmwage@talk.google.com as my JID. However it looks like to add another google/gmail user you have to use their username@gmail.com. This is an odd inconsistency between the JIDs that show up.. I wonder if they will fix this, or what. Doesn't look like s2s functionality is working, though. I tried to add cwage@jabber.centresource.com and I never got the request.
