Bad news: Looks like the US has fallen even farther behind in [broadband deployment:](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050523185420/http://www.telecomweb.com/news/1114631423.htm>)

> The United States has fallen yet further behind in broadband penetration, falling to 16th place in the world from 13th last year and 11th the year before, according to new numbers from the International Telecommunication Union (ITU). Of course, once upon a time - long before the ITU started keeping statistics on broadband - the United States was Number One.
> 
> The ITU did not issue any commentary along with its latest numbers, compiled as of Jan. 1. However, in a telling move, the international organization has expanded its annual listing of broadband penetration to encompass the top 20 countries in the world. In previous years, it had listed only the top 15. Had it continued with only 15, the United States would have dropped off the list entirely - relegated to the ignominious status of one of the countries in the ROW (rest of world) category.
