I love working with smart sarcastic people. And I hate working with cynics. True sarcasm is humor mixed with irony and wit. It sees the hilarity of the mundane and lifts peoples spirits by creating room to laugh. Cynics are people who have left sarcasm behind and embraced a lack of hope. No longer do they find the humor, but rather they suck the joy out of a room. So how do you carefully craft a culture of funny, witty people that care about each other, while crushing cynicism? Here are a few tips.

![Sarcasm-In-the-office](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/Sarcasm-Meter-94888964028-300x199.jpeg)

Hire for humor. In the interview process we often just look at the surface skills that someone brings to the table. But what about their sense of humor, their affability, their kindness? How can we draw conclusions after one interview? I recommend multiple interviews with several members of your team. I recommend telling a few jokes and seeing how the react. Be comfortable demonstrating the team's sense of humor as you collectively interview. Prospects will pick up on that and either recoil or join in the fun.

Leadership should take themselves less seriously. Leaders who wall themselves away either physically or emotionally (don't open their office door and don't hang out with the team) tend to foster a culture where levity is not valued. They usually communicate in a top down manner and only address the team to get some business done. Now granted we are all here for business, but you get more out of your team when you actually care about them. And to demonstrate that care you need to hang out, make yourself accessible and show that you can participate in the jokes floating around the office.

Keep it clean. Good sarcasm is not mean and isn't dirty. It's smart and insightful. The easiest way to cultivate this is to lead by example. Demonstrate how to be witty and nice at the same time. It's possible with a little intentionality.

While business comes first, you get more out of your team when you can demonstrate that you truly care about them. By promoting a culture of funny, witty people and crushing the cynics, you can make your office a more enjoyable, productive place.
