For years, I've heard about Dell's rock-solid support from all sorts of advertisements and propaganda. While I never heard about a Dell support experience from an actual person, I naturally assumed that their meteoric growth was from their support. Until I had my own support experiences...

I bought my first Dell in late 2003 - a shiny new Inspiron 600m. Within 3 months, my hard drive failed. I called support & was greeted by a support agent with a thick Indian accent. The agent was polite, but the call took forever... and they didn't really listen to me - just kept following the script. I wasn't particularly pleased, but I wasn't unhappy.

Since then, this scenario has repeated for other broken items such as:  
1) The 'X' key stopped working  
2) The laptop case cracked  
3) The keyboard had to be replaced for erratic key inputs

My last experience was DRAMATICALLY different. The touchpad started to wear out (and the left button stopped working), so I called support - expecting to go through another 2 hour call of methodical testing.

This time, I was greeted by a warm female voice in clear English. She asked a few short questions about my problem, scheduled a technician to repair my laptop, and then emailed me a support ticket. All of this took less than 10 minutes - incredible. The technician showed up on time, fixed the machine, and was very friendly.

Finally, the experience matches the hype!
