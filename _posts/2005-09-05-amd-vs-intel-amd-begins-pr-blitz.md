title: "AMD vs Intel: AMD begins PR blitz"
date: 2005-09-05
---
AMD continues to press their campaign against Intel. In addition to the lawsuit filed, AMD has [launched a website](<http://www.amd.com/breakfree>) devoted to telling their side of the story. This website is an innovative approach to 'getting the word out' about AMD and their match-up to Intel. They believe their chips are superior (especially when considering price), but Intel uses their monopolisitic powers to stifle their competion.

In addition to the website, AMD took an unusual approach by launching full page ads in several publications around the country. The NY Times [writes](<http://www.nytimes.com/2005/07/08/business/media/08adco.html?ex=1278475200&en=9fd0a0809470d01e&ei=5088&partner=rssnyt&emc=rss>):

> Besides the newspapers, the ad ran in both The Hill and Roll Call, two publications read by lawmakers and, presumably, antitrust investigators at the Federal Trade Commission. The Fair Trade Commission in Japan recently ruled against Intel over accusations that it stifled competition by offering rebates to five computer companies in exchange for their limiting their purchases from A.M.D. and Transmeta. So it is hardly surprising that Advanced Micro's lawyers would now want the attention of Capitol Hill.
