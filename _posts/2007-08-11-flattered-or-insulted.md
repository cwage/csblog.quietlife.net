date: 2007-08-11

For several years I was very involved in the 3d animation scene. In school I was a student volunteer (and team leader one year) at three SIGGRAPH conferences (2002, 2003, 2004). This morning, I came across this page: [FJORG!](<http://www.siggraph.org/s2007/attendees/fjorg/>). Fjorg! was a sort of iron-animator competition in which teams of animators had a set time during the conference to create a small animation. I won't get into the details here, if you are interested... check out the page.

The interesting thing about this contest is that I started the idea at the end of SIGGRAPH 2003 and spoke with several conference chairs about it at SIGGRAPH 2004 in an attempt to get the ball rolling. Several of them expressed interest in the project, but wanted to talk more after the conference. After 2004, I slowly got less involved with the industry as I got more involved in web development. The idea never left my mind, but I didn't actively seek to develop it because of the lowered contact I had with the animation community.

Fast forward to this morning... I see FJORG! on the SIGGRAPH 2007 site. The question running through my mind is, "should I be flattered, or should I be insulted?" The open side of me feels a little pride that I came up with the idea and planted it in the ears of the SIGGRAPH movers and shakers. The dark, sinister side of me wonders why nobody contacted me when they decided to proceed with the event. What do you think? Should I be content with sitting on the sidelines and watching my idea take flight, or should I feel slighted? Either way, congrats to the winning team from Bowling Green State University! One of the people that I really fleshed my idea out with was Bonnie Mitchell, the great 3d animation teacher right there at BGSU.
