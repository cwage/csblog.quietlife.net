Here at [CentreSource](<http://www.centresource.com/>), things have been exciting as we look for new team members to add to our small but growing firm. Like many firms before us (and many after), we have spent a great deal of time searching, qualifying, and interviewing potential candidates. It's critical we evaluate candidates on more than their technical skills - we need great team members! Unfortunately, ensuring a well-rounded candidate is not easy... but take hope! The Internet has (once again) revolutionized the way we, CentreSource, approach the hiring process.

With the rise of the Internet, services like [Monster.com](<http://www.monster.com/>) & [Dice.com](<http://www.dice.com/>) have become instrumental in providing a fluid job poster / finder resource. In our recent hunt, we posted a PHP Developer position with both services and had MANY job applicants with great resumes. However, we knew that a great team member needed a lot more than a great resume. We were amazed at how many people said they were 'experts' at a certain skill - but had very little (if any) real work experience.

After talking with other 'seasoned' entrepreneurs, I learned that we could pay firms to qualify candidates for prices ranging from $2o0-$500 per candidate. These firms would subject a candidate to a battery of tests based on an ideal profile we established (only costing us a measly $500 - $1000 to create the baseline).

Armed with the belief that personality testing would help us sift through the seemingly 'perfect' resumes - I searched the web for 'employee testing' and 'job match tests'. I reviewed various sites and landed on this little gem of a site: [http://www.brainbench.com](<http://www.brainbench.com/>). I couldn't believe that one site would have that many tests, of such a wide range, for such a reasonable cost... just $50 per online test.

For our candidates, we elected to use the 'Total Match - Programmer, PHP' test. Signing up gave us three free tests, so there was little risk to try the service out. One of our internal developers took the test (and did well might I add) and gave the green light for us to send to candidates. The test is intense & takes 2 hours to complete. The results give indication to factors around personality, problem solving, math/PHP/MySQL skills, and overall job match. It also allowed the candidate to 'solve' a problem with free-form code that we could review & determined their thought process.

Since our first experience, we've used the Brainbench assessments quite a bit. While we realize that a test is not the only indication of a candidates ability, it has been helpful in weeding out those that believe they are 'senior' developers when they are not. Beyond the technical skills, we also value the 'range' scores the assessment gives on factors such as work ethic, team work, and perfectionism. We value Team Work above many other personality factors - so a low score is a definite strike against a candidate.

Even for a small business, its clear how powerful the Internet has become in the role of Job Position fulfilling. The combination of job sites & candidate assessments gives CentreSource a great way to minimize the risk with hiring a new candidate - and for that we're grateful!
