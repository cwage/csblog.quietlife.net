![Unhappy customer](/content/uploads/2014/12/anger-management-221x300.jpg)Breaking News: Communication is the key to success in any relationship.

I know I didn't make this up, but it is especially true in the agency world and when a client is unhappy. Sometimes we all need a reminder to communicate with others what we discuss internally, and unfortunately that may come with an angry phone call or email from a client. Below are the five key steps to get a relationship with a client back on track:

  1. **Listen, listen, listen.** Many times the communication lines have been broken and a client is frustrated they haven't been heard. Before jumping into a reason, defense or apology, hear the other person out.
  2. **Transparency is key.** Budget, timeline and scope often become areas of frustration with clients. Maybe a timeline is on the verge of being missed, the estimate is higher than their budget or a piece of scope becomes more complex with time. When you trust your team you will feel confident sharing this information with the client immediately. There is always a resolution, but the earlier and more transparent you are with information the easier the resolution will be.
  3. **Say "Thank You."** Two words can go a long way. You never want clients to be angry, but when they are and come to you with their concerns you know there is a solid foundation to the relationship. Make sure to thank them for expressing their concern, their partnership, and their willingness to work towards a resolution.
  4. **Take it out of email.** We have become programmed to communicate almost exclusively through email. When you sense a change in tone or if you know your client is angry, the best thing to do is pick up the phone. I'd also recommend setting up a face to face meeting, if possible. Don't delay addressing their concerns.
  5. **Have a clear path of escalation.** There are times when you won't be able to resolve the situation, and that is okay. Know who you can escalate the client's concern to and have a clear framework in place.



**In an ideal world we will be able to predict and prevent clients from becoming angry. Until then, follow these 5 rules and I bet your relationships will live long and prosper (I also didn't make this up.)**
