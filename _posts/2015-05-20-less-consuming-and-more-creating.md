![Less Consuming. More Creating.](https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/562aa60ba4f4bf0d01f0d950a491630d-188x300.jpg)What if you consumed less than you created? I'm not talking about consuming less gasoline or taking shorter showers. I'm talking about, rather than constantly consuming blog articles, creating a blog article. Instead of browsing food blogs, create a blog of your own. For me, instead of downloading apps, create my own app. Once we stop consuming, that's when the world becomes a better place. Let me tell you what I mean.

What happens when we consume? Many feel productive. But that's a lie we tell ourselves. Did you read your Twitter feed? Check. Instagram feed? Check. RSS feed? Check. 30 minutes has passed but what have you actually accomplished from consuming all that content? Could you teach your co-worker how to step-by-step improve their SEO from the article you read? If you're like me, no you can't. All that time spent consuming and you're not any smarter and your day just got shorter. Not very productive.

What happens when we create? Your book, photo, recipe, tutorial or website did much more than you think. You've taught your readers something. You've made them smile. You've empowered them to do something they couldn't previously do. More than provide value to them, you've done a lot for yourself also.

You overcame a fear many artists suffer from: the fear to create and be judged for their work. We all know you could have perfected your work ad infinitum. I'll wager you learned more from creating that tutorial than reading a similar one. Additionally, you've now added a resource to your portfolio. In today's world, you will get Googled before you will get interviewed. The person that has 20 tutorials on web development will have a leg up over the person who has two years experience.

**The world needs more creators. It needs people that think, speak and create differently and share it with the world. Creating takes courage and time. It's really hard, but it's what the world needs. Once you create, opportunities will arise and I believe you'll be on a happier path as a creator than a consumer.**
