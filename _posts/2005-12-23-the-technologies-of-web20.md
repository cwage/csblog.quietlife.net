We found an article this week called [Web2.0 Tournament](<http://particletree.com/notebook/web-20-tournament/>). It points to many resources on the web that compare the technologies used to create web applications such as programming languages, databases, web servers, etc.

I have worked with a variety of web programming languages and really enjoy seeing other people's opinions of how they compare. The Web2.0 Tournament is a good resource and I hope Kevin continues to update it.

Some of the resources he linked to were really good while others are pretty laughable. The difficulty in creating good comparisons is that it is difficult to maintain proficiency in a wide variety of tools that serve the same purpose.

One thing is for sure, the technologies used by CentreSource fair really well in the Web2.0 Tournament.
