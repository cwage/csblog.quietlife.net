Check out what we've been up to lately! Press Release by the Young Leaders Council

NASHVILLE, Tenn., June 24, 2011--Young Leaders Council (YLC), a Nashville-based nonprofit organization that has trained more than 1,800 men and women to effectively participate on the boards of nonprofit agencies for the past 26 years, has announced its newly redesigned website donated by CentreSource, the interactive agency where YLC Class 44 alumnus Nicholas Holland is founder, president and CEO. The site at [www.ylcnashville.org](<http://www.ylcnashville.org/>) now includes an alphabetical listing of all YLC alumni since the first class met in 1985, an online program application, a sample class schedule, upcoming leadership luncheons, and close to 300 photos of class participants and events.

> "We are thrilled to have a professionally designed website that provides all the information needed for interested applicants or alumni who are committed to the mission of YLC," said Diane Hayes, executive director of YLC. "The generosity of the design by CentreSource is just another great example of what wonderful resources our alumni are for YLC."

"Personally, YLC was an amazing experience for myself," said Holland. "The lessons I learned, combined with the people I met demonstrated just how valuable the program is. I believe it is important that all alumni give back to keep YLC strong. CentreSource employs multiple YLC graduates and it was our pleasure to donate creative services that reflect the prestige of the program."

**About Young Leaders Council**

Since 1985, Young Leaders Council (YLC) has been the premier organization for training nonprofit board members in the Nashville area and was created to address the need to broaden and strengthen Nashville's volunteer leadership base. To date, more than 1,800 graduates of the training program have served on the boards of more than 150 nonprofit agencies. For more information about Young Leaders Council, visit the website at [www.ylcnashville.org](<http://www.ylcnashville.org/>).
