On or around the 10th of May, Sober.P suddenly stopped spreading and started checking some locations periodically, presumably to download a variant and continue spreading.

Yesterday, a new variation was detected, as expected, and was downloaded to these infected machines and has become active [as of today](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050524235741/http://www.viruslist.com/en/weblog?weblogid=163824967>).

This variant, however, rather than spreading, is simply sending out spam. Given the volume of proliferation that Sober.p enjoyed, this could be quite a headache.

[Swirbo](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050512010840/http://www.swirbo.com/>) is blocking this new variant, although it doesn't appear to be spreading via e-mail at this time.

**UPDATE:** The spam it's sending is evidently some sort of right-wing political messages in German. If you're getting spam or [backscatter](<https://csblog.quietlife.net/2005/05/03/backscatter/>) with these sorts of messages in German along with URLs to german sites, it's probably as a result of this. [SANS has more details.](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050519085405/http://isc.sans.org/diary.php?date=2005-05-15>)
