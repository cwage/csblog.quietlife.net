At a special Apple media event in San Francisco, Steve Jobs introduced several new models of iPod goodness for the holiday season. 

$79 for the Shuffle with remixed colors.  
$149 and $199 for a redesigned Nano with video.  
$249 and $399 for iPod classic, thinner and more capacity - 80GB at a 30GB price.  
$499 and $599 for the iPhone - unchanged.  
$299 and $399 for iPod Touch with built-in WiFi - basically an iPhone minus the phone.

As with all Apple announcements, the best is saved for last...

What! An iPhone price drop... you're kidding me!... The 4GB iPhone at $499 is no more. A 8GB iPhone is NOW $399! 

Unbelievable - a TWO HUNDRED DOLLAR PRICE DROP inside of sixty days.

Apple iPhone early adopters are wailing from coast to coast. Meanwhile, a new crop of customers are without question, ready to open their wallets. 

While Apple may deal with a momentary public relations fallout from the price drop, the greater pressure will be on the handset manufacturers like Palm, Samsung, Motorola and RIM to compete on the feature sets and user experience of the iPhone. Despite the limitations of the iPhone, no other smart phone has captured the imagination of the consumer like the iPhone. And isn't capturing one's imagination the basis of great marketing?
