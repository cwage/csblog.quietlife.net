Everyone loves [Gmail](<http://www.gmail.com/>)... so the fact that they are letting you use their service as a storage medium is really icing on the cake. Both windows & Linux users have a pretty easy method of transferring files to & from your Gmail account - directly from your local machine. Here are some resources to get you started.

**Windows XP Users:**  
Engadget gives [a great tutorial (How-To) on mounting Gmail](<http://www.engadget.com/entry/1234000640033887/>) in your Windows XP machine. You need a drive extension, but its pretty easy after that.

**Linux Users:**  
Richard Jones (who also has hacks for Google's [Cartography system](<http://richard.jones.name/google-hacks/google-cartography/google-cartography.html>) & [S/MIME features](<http://richard.jones.name/google-hacks/gmail-smime/gmail-smime.html>)) has a [great post](<http://richard.jones.name/google-hacks/gmail-filesystem/gmail-filesystem.html>) with files links for adding GmailFS to your Linux machine.
