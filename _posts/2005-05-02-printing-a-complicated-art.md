I just finished a conversation with a print company offering their services to CentreSource. We have recently completed the design of our marketing materials and are now in the market for someone to print them. As I began researching this subject, I've quickly learned that the printing industry is as complicated as any other technical industry I've encountered!

1 Color, 2 Color, 3 Colors, 4 Color Mapping / Spotting, Screens, Digital Printing!  
Bi-fold, Tri-fold, Ink Bleeds to Edge, Special cutting, Print Runs!

Its enough to make a guys head explode. Now I know how our clients feel when we try to talk with them. That's what I went with a referral from someone I trust: [http://www.sbforms.net](<http://www.sbforms.net/>). Linda Berry was very informative as she tried to explain her world to a non-printer 'type' :)

Ultimately, the process is this:

**1) Design your own work (Corel Draw/Illustrator/Freehand)**  
** You can pay a designer to do it as well. Prices range from $50-$100/hour. It can go even higher if you're looking for a complete Brand Strategy. We, fortunately, are skilled at [Branding and Design](<http://www.centresource.com/cd_graphicdesign>) \- so we made our own!

**2) Determine the details (Copies, Paper Type, Colors, and Special Fold/Cuts)**  
** This will vary once you see the prices! From our research, prices for 250 were almost the same as for 500. You can even get 1000+ for marginally more. It seems the bulk cost comes from getting those presses running!

Each color count (1,2,3,4+) increases the cost significantly. In fact, going from 2 colors to 3 colors often increased the price 50%! We opted for standard 100# weight white gloss paper, folded in half.

**Note:** Having your ink 'bleed' to the edges also raises the cost. This basically means: is your print item a color other than white? If so, do you want that color to go to edge of the paper? That is considered bleeding to the edge.

**3) Setup time for Proofs**  
** You can pay approximately $20/item to get a proof of your piece (highly recommended). While not 100% exact, it will give you a good idea of what your work will look like on paper.

**4) Approve the print and wait 7-10 days for your final prints!**

_We were also offered 'Digital Printing' for a smaller print run. This allows for 100-500 prints and costs less than traditional printing. As a downside, its a lower quality - albeit sometimes imperceptible to many._

* * *

Cost estimates for a small company

  * Design / Layout: $250 - $1000 per item, depending on complexity
  * Printing of 1000 Copies: $300 [3-Color, Edge Bleeding, Glossy, Bi-fold]
