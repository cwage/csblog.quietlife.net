Through the tragic natural disasters in Japan and last year's major earthquake in Haiti, the interactive world has stepped up to the plate and become a key component to humanitarian work and relief efforts. Two specific applications, [Google People Finder](<http://japan.person-finder.appspot.com/?lang=en>) and [Ushahidi](<http://www.ushahidi.com/>), have allowed the interactive world to become a part of relief efforts by providing interactive mapping and information collecting with zero cost to the user.

According the staff at Ushahidi, they are a core platform built on that gathering crisis information from the general public provides new insights into events happening in near real-time. Ushahidi offers products that enable local observers to submit reports using their mobile phones or the internet, while simultaneously creating an archive of events. Here is a short animated video explaining the process:

[Changing the World with Ushahidi](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vJLbVAWWPMA&feature=relmfu>)

Google Person Finder is a web application that provides a registry and message board for survivors, family and loved ones affected by a natural disaster. Family members and survivors can post and search information about each others status and location. Less than 12 hours after the large earthquake in Japan, 7,200 records had been tracked. The software widget is very user friendly by only providing two buttons, "I'm looking for someone" and "I have information about someone".

I'd like to applaud the interactive world for developing such world impactful tools!
