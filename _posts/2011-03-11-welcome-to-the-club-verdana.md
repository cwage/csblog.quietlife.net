As a designer, I've always had a love of fonts since I can really remember. Its become quite the obsession lately and I find myself acting giddy when I come across a beautiful font such as Mrs. Eaves or misjudging a business that uses fonts such as Comic Sans or Papyrus. So, you can imagine my excitement when I found out there is a so called 'Fonts Hall of Fame' at The Museum of Modern Art. On March 2nd, MoMA acquired 23 digital typefaces for its [Architecture and Design Collection](<http://moma.org/explore/collection/architecture_design>).

You may recognize a few of these 'hall of famer' fonts such as Gotham, Verdana, or Temple Gothic. Each font was chosen based on historical relevance, social significance and functionality. However, something that sets these particular fonts apart from others is that each of the 23 fonts nominated are all **_digital fonts_** or designed because of the digital revolution.

The 23 acquired typefaces are:  
• American Type Founders **[OCR-A](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139567>) **(1966)  
• Wim Crouwel [**New Alphabet**](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139322>) (1967)  
• Matthew Carter **[Bell Centennial](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139307>) **(1976-78)  
• Matthew Carter [**ITC** ](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139306>)**[Galliard](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139306>) **(1978)  
• Erik Spiekermann [**FF Meta**](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139324>) (1984-1991)  
• Zuzana Licko **[Oakland](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139320>) **(1985)  
• Jeffery Keedy **[Keedy Sans](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139318>) **(1991)  
• Erik van Blokland and Just van Rossum **[FF Beowolf](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139326>) **(1990)  
• Barry Deck **[Template Gothic](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139319>) **(1990)  
• P. Scott Makela **[Dead History](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139317>) **(1990)  
• Jonathan Hoefler **[HTF Didot](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139304>) **(1991)  
• Neville Brody **[FF Blur](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139325>) **(1992)  
• Jonathan Barnbrook **[Mason](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139321>) **(1992)  
• Matthew Carter **[Mantinia](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139309>) **(1993)  
• Tobias Frere-Jones **[Interstate](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139305>) **(1993-95)  
• Matthew Carter **[Big Caslon](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139308>) **(1994)

• Matthew Carter [**Miller**](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139310>) (1997)  
• Jonathan Hoefler & Tobias Frere-Jones [**Retina**](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139302>) (1999)  
• Jonathan Hoefler & Tobias Frere-Jones **[Gotham](<http://www.moma.org/collection/browse_results.php?object_id=139301>) **(2000)

Here's to digital fonts making their stamp on typographical history!
