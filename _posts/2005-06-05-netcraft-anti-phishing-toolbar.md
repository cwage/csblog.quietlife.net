[Phishing](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050608090742/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phishing>), in recent months, is gaining notoriety due to its link to financial loss for its victims. While many [anti-spam services](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050522012737/http://www.swirbo.com/>) will stop phishing emails, even one making it through can be devastating to a user.

Moreover, [Pharming ](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050606231608/http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharming>)completely bypasses the email system - rendering any Anti-Spam service (like [Swirbo](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050522012737/http://www.swirbo.com/>)) useless to stop the problem.

[Netcraft](<https://web.archive.org/web/20080827232325/http://www.netcraft.com/>), an Internet security firm, released a [toolbar ](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050607000401/http://toolbar.netcraft.com/>)for both Internet Explorer and [Firefox ](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050606014259/http://www.mozilla.org/>)to combat phishing/pharming (site redirection / mimicry).

**General Review**  
For Firefox 1.x, the installation was very simple (most extensions for firefox are) and didn't require any personal information.

Once you restart the browser, you'll notice a new toolbar with the Netcraft logo and accompanying information. My first impression was that too much space was used by the bar. While it's on marginally wider than my [A9 toolbar](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050606011613/http://toolbar.a9.com/>), it did take up an entire row while only providing 5 pieces of information. Call me a stickler for space...

The information provided is the following:

  * Risk Rating - This is the simplest representation of the danger related to the site you're viewing. For every site I checked, it had the full green meter (thus, were perfectly safe) 
  * Since - Gives you a quick view of how old the site is. Phishing sites will normally be relatively recent.
  * Rank - Provides a Netcraft specific rank of the sites visited by its users. Their site says the information is anonymously sent to the ranking servers.
  * Hosting - This was my favorite feature. It displays the hosting company of the website you're visiting. CentreSource works with a lot of hosting companies, so I found it very fascinating to see what other companies chose for their hosting. **Note: I did not find this particularly helpful on the phishing side. ThePlanet & iPowerWeb are two hosting companies we've used - and they are listed as some of the ['phishiest hosters'](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050607000401/http://toolbar.netcraft.com/stats/hosters>) on the web.**



The links on the toolbar will take you to a Site Report which displays items such as IP Address, Country, Organization, Hosting Company, Nameserver, DNS Admin, and even hosting history (showing the history of this site).

**Active Monitoring**

The most important feature is the auto-protecting features of the toolbar. For the basic user, the Netcraft toolbar will actually block phishing related websites. In addition to blocking sites, a user can also report phishing sites (which are reviewed and then disseminated to the other toolbar users for their protection).

Overall, its a good start for a security measure. Unfortunately, I am reluctant to give up my screen space for something that may NEVER provide any value (its like insurance... no one is ever happy to pay for it). For me, I'll probably abstain from using it and just take precaution. For other users (namely those that don't use any other toolbars, I think its a good thing to have).

In the future, I hope Netcraft integrates something useful like a popup blocker and integrated search with [Google](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050606014752/http://toolbar.google.com/>)/[Yahoo ](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050406181543/http://toolbar.yahoo.com/>)\- this will make the toolbar complete and eliminates my need for other toolbars.
