Sometime in 2012, I made the decision to invest in a standing desk. After seeing [terrifying headlines](<http://www.theatlantic.com/health/archive/2012/04/confirmed-he-who-sits-the-most-dies-the-soonest/256101/>) like "He Who Sits the Most Dies the Soonest", I wanted to make a better health choice for myself.

I'm also on my feet a lot anyway--I spend a lot of time consulting with others in the building, speaking in meetings, or brainstorming in front of a whiteboard. Having a standing desk meant a simpler transition from my desk to other areas in the office; it meant less time spent "context switching". It seemed like a great move.

Custom-built standing desks, while [beautiful](<http://www.focaluprightfurniture.com/>), can be outrageously expensive when [compared](<http://www.wired.com/wiredscience/2012/10/mf-standing-desk/>) to the DIY route.

I found [this handy article](<http://iamnotaprogrammer.com/Ikea-Standing-desk-for-22-dollars.html>) which detailed a simple, cheap way to modify an existing desk (which is what I had) into a standing option. $22 dollars cheap, no less. Unfortunately, I didn't want to modify my desk - the bolt-on Frankenstein desk looks too strange to me. I wanted something that matched our office decor and allowed me to stand at work.

![Standing Desk Best Practices](https://skitch-img.s3.amazonaws.com/20120619-qqtq8nf6agxxk29cg9bsih8biq.png)

_The perfect chart to measure the components you'll need for your desk._

I started digging through [Ikea Hackers] and found [several](<http://www.ikeahackers.net/2011/01/wide-standing-desk.html>) good options. Ultimately I settled on [this example](<http://www.ikeahackers.net/2012/05/standing-desk-with-utby-legs.html>), which, of course, was discontinued. I took to [Ikea's site](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/>) and pieced together my own configuration:

Here's the component list:

  * [Vika Amon table top](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/00209839/>) \- $10.99
  * [Ekby Järpen shelf top](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/20025047/#/20094347>) \- $14.99
  * [Vika Byske table leg (x4)](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/84609085/>) \- $30.00 ($120 for 4)
  * [Capita shelf legs](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/20049538/>) (mine are the 8", but if you're shorter you might want the 6") - $14.00
  * [Franklin stool](<http://www.ikea.com/us/en/catalog/products/50199215/>) (Again, I got the taller option) - $39.99



I also bought myself a simple [anti-fatigue mat](<http://www.amazon.com/Genuine-Joe-Anti-Fatigue-Beveled-3-Feet/dp/B000EFK9KM/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1359243126&sr=8-1&keywords=standing+desk+mat>), which lives discreetly under an area rug in our office. It makes the seasoned wood floors in our office a bit more manageable for long hours.

All in, a standing desk that cost less than $200 and comes in multiple color and finish options. The Vika Byske legs are incredible, in that they can be adjusted to fit a user up to about 6'6" in height. Pretty robust.

The result? I hear "Wow, you're tall!" a lot in the office (I'm 6'2"). Standing all day has made my posture better, my focus improved, and while it was a bit tiring at first, I'm definitely noticing some improved health benefits. It takes some getting used to, but now I may never sit again. Here's the final product in action:
