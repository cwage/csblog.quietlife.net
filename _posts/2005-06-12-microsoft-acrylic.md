I am late in posting on this, but a friend of mine at Microsoft has finally released a beta of the product he's been working on, [Microsoft Acrylic](<http://www.microsoft.com/products/expression/>). It looks pretty interesting, though it's getting some [nasty reviews](<http://slashdot.org/articles/05/06/11/1851231.shtml?tid=152&tid=109&tid=185>) on Slashdot. Though, it's unclear what exactly this guy's credentials to review it are, as he compares it to Photoshop throughout the review, despite the fact that Acrylic is not even remotely designed to compete with Photoshop. As one commenter on Slashdot noted about the review:

> And you expect better from a guy who spends the first page describing 3 different reasons why his inability to execute an HTTP download is a problem of the software he hasn't even installed yet (even throwing in an evil Microsoft conspiracy one liner)? 

His anti-Microsoft bias is of ridiculous proportions, and it's sad that slashdot considers this sort of thing news-worthy. Anyways, check out the beta. It looks interesting -- I plan on trying it out as soon as I can find time to reboot into Windows. One of these days.
