Continuing with my [support of PHP](<http://blog.centresource.com/2005/06/06/defending-php-mysql-lamp/>), I've gathered articles comparing PHP 4.x/5 to Microsoft's ASP.NET. Its important to note that .NET is a framework & ASP is the web version of a .NET program, whereas PHP is an actual language. ASP.NET can actually be written in a variety of languages such as C#, VB.NET, etc.

As one of Nashville's PHP Development firms, we encounter clients asking us the merits of ASP.NET vs PHP. Typically, this arises when we are competing for a project with a Microsoft development shop. What we've come to realize is that both platforms and languages can get the job done - its just a matter of:

  1. How fast
  2. How expandable
  3. How much



If you want to take our word for it (without reading further), our final analysis deems that both platforms are viable options. For a fresh beginning, ASP.NET will incur significant additional costs in hosting and licensing fees upon deployment. This makes the ROI lower on initial projects. Initial projects is a clear distinctions because a client may be able to host multiple applications on a single hosted environment - so the costs are not linear. In contrast, they hosting/licensing costs for a LAMP (Linux Apache MySQL PHP/Perl) installation are much reduced - thus leading to a higher ROI.

_A forum post on SitePoint explains it very well when they say, "Its like comparing French and Spanish. You can say the same thing in both languages... neither is really superior to the other. However, if you're in Spain - you should speak Spanish!" Translation: They can both make the application you want, but the real decision will come from your support & infrastructure environments. If you have great PHP developers on hand, then you'll go that way. If you have a windows server with all the software you need, you may go the ASP.NET way. The debate rages on..._
