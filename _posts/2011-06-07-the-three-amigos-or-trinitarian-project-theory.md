[![3 triangles](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/3-triangles-300x266.png)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/3-triangles.png> "3 triangles")No matter what you are building, planning, or designing, three key points will always need to be addressed; Scope, Timeline, and Budget. Don't believe me? Try to make something without any scope and you will wander aimlessly, never arriving at a finished product. Ignore budget and watch yourself go out of business. And there is not a client on the planet who will hire you to do anything without a timeline. So how do you define these items?

Web sites and mobile applications have an exorbitant number of moving pieces, and we've found that the only way to deal with them all is through a detailed and intentional process. At CS, we tackle all three with a Strategy and Planning engagement. We bring an entire team to meet with our clients so that right from day one, we have the insight of a strategist, a developer, and a designer, focused on defining and delivering our client's goals. With this intensive approach, we can flesh out the details of our Scope, Timeline, and Budget. But what happens if one of them changes along the way?

In every project you need to figure out which of these key points is the master. For instance, if Budget is driving decisions, Scope will have to be carefully limited to make sure you deliver the project within the pricing guidelines. Once you know the master, and confirm that your client agrees with your assessment, let them help you manage the project to completion. If they want to change the Scope, remind them that Budget and Timeline will need to be adjusted accordingly. If they want to move launch up by two weeks, have the same conversation. The triangular relationship between these three ensures that if you change the boundaries of any single item, you change the others as well. If you manage all three components well, the end result is a happy client and a happy firm.
