I might be the only person at Centresource with this vintage gem in my pocket:

[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Photo-on-2011-06-30-at-11.13.jpg)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Photo-on-2011-06-30-at-11.13.jpg> "Photo on 2011-06-30 at 11.13")

I always try to stand up for my little Samsung electric blue phone by saying things like "but it has a keyboard!" and "it slides open and I can take a photo!". Sometimes when I'm in a group of my fellow centresource friends, I like to pretend I can tap it and scroll up and down. Who am I fooling?

However, if I had an iphone (and I secretly wish I did), these are the Apps I would love to have as a web designer:

1\. [**Palettes** :](<http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/palettes/id297157708?mt=8>) You can create a new color palette at anytime and grab colors from a photograph, a website, or add colors using any one of 5 color models. How helpful would that be when you don't happen to have a camera around your neck or photoshop at your fingers?

[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Screen-shot-2011-06-30-at-11.22.11-AM.png)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Screen-shot-2011-06-30-at-11.22.11-AM.png> "Screen shot 2011-06-30 at 11.22.11 AM")

2\. [CSS Cheat Sheet](<http://www.concentricsky.com/products/iphone/css-cheat-sheet/>) : We all need a little help sometimes, even if you're an experienced web developer or designer. How nice would it be to have a CSS cheat sheet on your phone to use as a quick reference for topics you've already learned but need a few reminders on?

[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Screen-shot-2011-06-30-at-11.27.42-AM-209x300.png)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/Screen-shot-2011-06-30-at-11.27.42-AM.png> "Screen shot 2011-06-30 at 11.27.42 AM")

3\. [What The Font](<http://new.myfonts.com/WhatTheFont/iPhone/>) : Lets just face the facts, about 50% of the reason I like this app is because 'What the Font?' is fun to say. Also, if you're a designer like me that loves fonts but can't always reach back to Typography 101, you need a little font recognition help sometimes! Ever seen a great font in a magazine ad, poster, or on the web and wondered what font it is? Whip out your iPhone and snap a photo, and WhatTheFont for iPhone will identify that font in seconds! PS: This app is FREE.

[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/fonts-phone-240-140x300.png)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/fonts-phone-240.png> "fonts-phone-240")

4\. [Camera Bag](<http://itunes.apple.com/app/camerabag/id291176178?mt=8>) : I'll be honest, I'd love to jump on the 'lets make our photos look awesome by using the Helga feature" photo train.

[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/mzl.ahtatxqv.320x480-75-200x300.jpg)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/06/mzl.ahtatxqv.320x480-75.jpg> "mzl.ahtatxqv.320x480-75")

What are your favorite apps out there? Maybe someday I'll join the cool kids club!
