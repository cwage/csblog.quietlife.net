You know, I [made a joke](<http://blog.centresource.com/2005/06/27/rss-in-iewindows/>) about streamlining Windows vulnerabilities with its new support of RSS, but maybe I wasn't as [far off the mark](<http://www.techweb.com/wire/security/164903095>) as I thought:

> "What inevitably happens with any new protocol, especially the ones with the word "simple" in them, is that developers try to come up with a way to easily communicate data," said Pescatore. "Only at the end do they say, 'let's sprinkle some security on it.' RSS is like that." 

To be fair, I think this is a little premature alarmism. RSS is just XML. "Securing" it is up to who/what is using it.
