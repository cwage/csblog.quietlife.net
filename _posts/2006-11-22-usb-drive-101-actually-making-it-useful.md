I'm now the proud owner of a 2GB USB Drive & I wanted to share a few tips/tricks I've learned for getting the most use from it! I want my USB to provide a self-contained computing experience that I can take with me. When I pop it in, I want everything that I'm comfortable with at my finger tips :) I'll help you setup your USB drive to address the following needs (no order):

  1. Sensitive Data Storage - Examples include financial info, passwords, etc.
  2. Email Access - Allowing you to comfortably check your mail from anywhere
  3. Synchronization - As storage amounts increase, more data can be backed up
  4. Buddies/Contacts - Instant messaging to friends is available anywhere
  5. Office & Productivity - Work on docs, spreadsheets, and presentations



## First things first, get a USB launcher

The Ativa drive I bought at Office Max for $45 (on special) came with "[U3 Technology](<http://software.u3.com/interstitial.aspx>)" for running apps directly from the drive. It was nice, but I ultimately decided to use a replacement from [PortableApps](<http://portableapps.com/>). Unfortunately, U3 doesn't make it obvious how to uninstall and I had to search to [find the answer](<http://www.u3.com/uninstall/>). Once it was uninstalled, I downloaded the [PortableApps Standard Suite](<http://portableapps.com/suite>) and followed the [installation instructions](<http://portableapps.com/support/portable_apps_suite#installing>). This suite killed two birds with one stone - I had an application 'launcher' & I had some cool applications. U3 gave me the launcher and then made me individually seek out the cool applications.

With this suite installed, I had the office piece (Open Office Portable) & the Email piece (Thunderbird). I opened Thunderbird and entered in my email server details [Note: I don't recommend doing this if you have POP3 unless you 'Leave a copy on the server'. IMAP users - you're in a good boat with me!)

The PortableApps site also provided free add-on solutions for IM ([GAIM](<http://portableapps.com/apps/internet/gaim_portable>)) and Music/Video ([VLC Media Player](<http://portableapps.com/apps/music_video/vlc_portable>))

## Next, Sensitive Data Protection

Interestingly enough, neither PortableApps nor U3 provided a good solution for sensitive data. U3 provides an app called Roboforms, but I couldn't figure it out to save my life. Thanks to a very [helpful article from TipMonkies](<http://www.tipmonkies.com/2005/06/19/keep-passwords-safe-with-keepass/>), I learned about [KeePass](<http://keepass.sourceforge.net/>) \- a little password application for storing sensitive data on your USB drive. Since it doesn't come with either suite, you have to follow these steps to install:

  1. Download the [ZIP version](<http://superb-west.dl.sourceforge.net/sourceforge/keepass/KeePass-1.06.zip>) (the .exe version will install to your PC)
  2. Open your USB drive and create the folder X:\PortableApps\KeePass
  3. Unzip the downloaded file and copy/paste the .exe file into the new folder
  4. Re-start the PortableApp Launcher and then select KeePass from the menu



## Other goodies to consider...

Alas, there are other great apps you can put on your USB drive. Following the same manual steps listed above, I added these applications:

  * [XnView](<http://www.download.com/XnView/3000-2192_4-10067391.html>) \- Super Image viewer/editor that's light weight and fast!
  * [Abstract Spoon's ToDoList](<http://www.abstractspoon.com/tdl_resources.html>) \- Simple ToDo List that's really small
  * File Restoration (REST2514.EXE) - Originally created by [Brian Kato](<http://web.archive.org/web/20030624201736/http://hccweb1.bai.ne.jp/~hcj58401/>) and now provided by [Kelly](<http://www.kellys-korner-xp.com/regs_edits/REST2514.EXE>)
  * Security (and Encryption) - [TrueCrypt](<http://www.truecrypt.org/>) wins hands down (Read the FAQ!)



## Last, let's back it all up

I saved this section for last because it actually took me a little while to settle on which back up method I wanted to use. After thinking about it, I want to 'Synchronize' my PC with my USB - not use either as a backup. Synchronization and Backup are very different in certain ways. If you want a free back up solution, [here is a great page with many options](<http://www.hsinlin.com/software/backup.html>). If you want a synchronization solution, I recommend [Allway Sync](<http://www.allwaysync.com/>).

There's a lot here, but its all pretty simple to download and install. When you're done, you'll have a pretty functional USB drive - something you can plug in just about anywhere and start using! Good luck!
