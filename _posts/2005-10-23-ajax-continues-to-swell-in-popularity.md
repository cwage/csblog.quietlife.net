It feels good to know the future ;) For the past 2 years, I've been touting the beauty of web-based software and how it will truly revolutionize the computing world. A few months ago, my passion for web applications increased when I heard the buzz growing for AJAX (and consequently, saw some great uses of it beyond Google Maps).

Here's a [great article from Yahoo!News](<http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20051023/ap_on_hi_te/software_on_the_web;_ylt=AuJhd4zLmu0NAB4uvs6nLbkjtBAF;_ylu=X3oDMTA5aHJvMDdwBHNlYwN5bmNhdA-->) that discusses the swell of popularity in AJAX - and also mentions new technology coming from Microsoft to do even more.

The final part of the article addresses some real concerns with Web Applications. If the transaction volume is high, you can end up losing some efficiency due to the delays when comparing web apps to local apps. However, I think the whole point is that AJAX is another step in the right direction to minimize refresh inefficiencies.

All in all, my beliefs point to the internet and centralized computing as the way of the future. Granted, there are things that will need a local app - but even those can be displayed and presented through a web application.

The reason?

Web Applications merely represent the display method an application uses to interface with a user. The back-end processing can occur on any type/size/cluster of machines. More importantly, web technologies like flash allow even more apps to be web based.

Design, Games, and CAD can have their desktops - the rest of the world will want the convenience of a web app :)
