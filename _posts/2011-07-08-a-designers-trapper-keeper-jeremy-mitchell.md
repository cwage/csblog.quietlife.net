**1\. What's in your trapper keeper these days?**

1\. [Pinterest.com](<http://www.pinterest.com/jeremymitchell>) I find inspiration by looking at photography and product design. The colors and shapes speak to me. I used to look at other web gallery sites for inspiration. But I feel like everything starts looking the same. Looking at photography and product design is a nice break from just looking at other websites.

2\. The Perch in Brentwood: This is one of my favorite places to sit and work from. It's quiet and I can think there. Their crepes and coffee are amazing. And the people are super nice.

3\. [Vimeo.com](<http://www.vimeo.com/>) I like to watch the staff picks. Watching a well done video clears my mind and sparks ideas.

4\. Pandora Radio + iPhone + Apple Airport Express: Having music playing while I'm working is key. It sets the mood for the entire day. Having it on my iPhone, played through the speakers via AirPort Express is key. 

5\. Wired Magazine (iPad app): The design and the technology behind this app are very inspiring to me. (The articles are good too.) 

6\. HGTV: My wife and I watch a ton of HGTV. The design techniques translate well into print and web design.

**2\. What are your inspirations?**

1\. Everytime I go to Southern California, I instantly feel inspired.

2\. I love hearing about an amazing success story. Hearing about other's successes inspires me in my own ideas. 

3\. HGTV's Curb Appeal the Block and Uncover Boss inspire me. That may sound super cheesy, but it's true.

4\. Last and definitely not least, my wife inspires me the most.

**3\. When designing, you feel____?**

Anywhere from excited to discouraged. Mostly excited.

**4\. What's your favorite color?**

About 4 different shades of grey. I would give you the hex values but they are a trade secret. Seriously I think grey is pretty great.

**5\. How do you continue to build your design 'tool belt'?**

Twitter and keeping up with blogs and sites like Pinterest. Also, keeping up with new up and coming technology.

**6\. Do you have any advice to future designers?**

****

Be your own client. Build a website for yourself. Not just a portfolio site, but something totally unexpected. If you have an idea for a product, go for it. Design a t-shirt that you would wear. Create a poster that you would frame and hang on your wall. You have nothing to loose. And you just might have fun doing it.
