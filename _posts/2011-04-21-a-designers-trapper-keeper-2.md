I'm always fascinated with other designer's tricks of the trades so when I saw [Design Sponge](<http://www.designspongeonline.com/category/whats-in-your-toolbox>) feature a 'What's in Your Tool Box" series, I knew it would be fun for our designers here at centresource to showcase our tools, or in other words, show a designer's trapper keeper.****

**1\. What's in your graphic design 'Trapper Keeper' these days?**

**[](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/whats-in-your-tool-box1.jpg>)[![](http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/whats-in-your-tool-box2.jpg)](<http://blog.centresource.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/04/whats-in-your-tool-box2.jpg>)  
**

1\. Mac Book Pro 2. Asus external monitor 3. User interface design stencils 4. Pandora radio 5. Ear buds to listen to music and help "get in the zone" 6. A great sketch book! 7. Green tea to help me stay alert

**2\. What are your inspirations?**

I think we live in an age where its really easy to be inspired because there is so much art at our finger tips with the internet. I'm constantly inspired by creative blogs such as [Design Sponge](<designspongeonline.com>), [Swiss Miss](<http://www.swiss-miss.com/>), and [Poppy Talk](<http://poppytalk.blogspot.com/>). I feel really fortunate to live in Nashville where I'm among creatives every day whether it be musicians, photographers, designers or sewers. It seems as though everywhere I look I feel inspired because of the beautiful work that comes out of this town...just look at [Hatch Show Print](<http://countrymusichalloffame.org/our-work/>) for an example! ****

**3\. When designing, you feel_____?**

I feel equal parts excited, in my own world, pressure to succeed, and lucky that I get to create pieces of art for the internet.

**4\. What's your favorite color?**

When I was young, I would say yellow was my favorite color because I felt sorry for it. No one ever says they truly love yellow. Nowadays, mustard/harvest yellow is actually my new favorite color.

**5\. How do you continue to build your design 'tool belt'?**

I'm currently doing a heavy load of CSS/HTML tutorials that is making me feel like I'm trying to drink water from a fire hydrant! I love it though! I also would some day love to take a screen printing class or go back to the basics with a painting class.

**6\. Do you have any advice to future designers?**

I once heard the saying "the devil is in the details". Its so true! Make sure you focus on the details because that is what will set apart your design from other designs out there. Also, remember to have fun with it and _enjoy_ the whole process! Its so fun to have a career where you can see the fruits of your labor in front of you or in your hands.**
