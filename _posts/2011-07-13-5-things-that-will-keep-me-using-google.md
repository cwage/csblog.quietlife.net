1. [Hang Out ](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QN38vHZjWXw&feature=relmfu>)\- As a new Nashville resident and a Cincinnati native, I make every effort to connect with my family back home. Recently my family had a hang out session, connecting us across 3 states. Seeing my parents and two brothers all on one video chat was pretty amazing. The camera switching feature (it changes who's on camera based on who's talking) at first takes you off guard, but once understood the functionality won me over. This will not be the last time we use this feature.
  2. [Circles](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ocPeAdpe_A8&feature=relmfu>) - This savvy design has me thoroughly engaged. It is so intuitive. So simple.
  3. [Android's Google + app](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N8gvHaX1cwY&feature=player_embedded>) - This app has already beat every Facebook application I have seen on any Smartphone platform. The live stream, photos and contacts are expected basic requirement, but the app has so much more to offer. The ability to start a "huddle" or group text of up to 50 people on the go is awesome. These push notifications remove the need for SMS, utilizing the phones data network. Plus I can now upload photos automatically on the go, in a special folder. The Profile section of the app is what linkedin has been trying to do in mobile for quite some time. All in all the Google + app for Android is very impressive, with multiple elements from other social networks (Facebook, Linkedin, and Twitter.)
  4. [Google +1 Integration](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OAyUNI3_V2c&feature=relmfuhttp://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OAyUNI3_V2c&feature=relmfu>) - Google gives us the option to enable this option, which seems a bit big brother-ish but then again so did Facebook Likes. Google describes this setting as: "Allow Google to use my +1's and other profile information to personalize content and ads on non-Google websites. I may see +1 recommendations from people I know when visiting those sites." Having the ability to turn this off at anytime does ease any hesitation I may have around this.
  5. [Settings](<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vnv1Mbj1jKw&feature=relmfu>) - I love the ability to choose who sees each one of my posts. I really dig this feature. By selecting which groups can view each post, I can post feeds based off my followers interest. So I can post information, links or other content uniquely for my coworkers, friends and family.



While I have only been using Google Plus for a few days, I can honestly say these features will keep this from becoming "just a fad" for me. This is by no means a replacement for Facebook for me yet, but it has some potential.
