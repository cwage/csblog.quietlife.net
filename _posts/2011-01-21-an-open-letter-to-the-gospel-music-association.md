[![](https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/Dove-Awards.jpg)](<https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/01/Dove-Awards.jpg> "Dove-Awards")I was recently invited to judge the Recorded Music Packaging category for this year's Gospel Music Association Dove Awards. I have done this for a few years now and am truly honored to review such amazing designs. I can't go into specifics on which ones I voted for, but I will say, some of this year's nominees were super inspiring! 

The Gospel Music Association serves as the face and voice for the Christian/Gospel music community and is dedicated to exposing, promoting and celebrating the gospel through music of all styles including pop, rock, praise & worship, country, and many more. The GMA community consists of over 4,000 members which includes agents, artists, church leaders, radio personnel, record company executives and other industry visionaries.

I am a BIG FAN and encourage anyone in the music industry to become a member and get involved!

However, my one beef with music related awards in general is that the interactive industry is still ignored. As the industry moves towards more online purchases and less physical CD sales, an artist's web presence is more important than ever. I strongly believe that these web designers should be acknowledged and recognized for their contributions and talent.

I will be submitting a letter to the GMA Board proposing the addition of a category entitled "Interactive Marketing Website of the Year". I have included the letter below:

> To Whom It May Concern,
> 
> It is after much thought and consideration that we come to you, on behalf of the community of web designers and interactive media professionals, to propose a new ballot category for the annual GMA Dove Awards.
> 
> The category proposed is Interactive Marketing Website of the Year.
> 
> While photographers, production companies, designers, and art directors are included in the Recorded Music Packaging and Short and Long Form Music Video categories, web and interactive designers unfortunately are left out. The addition of the Interactive Marketing Website of the Year award would expand the GMA's reach to this growing and highly influential community.
> 
> The qualification for nomination would be equivalent to the qualifications for Recorded Music Packaging and Short/Long Form Music Video, artistically speaking, while taking into consideration an artist's brand packaging for ALL production - websites and interactive media included and not forgotten.
> 
> We are steadily becoming a primarily "internet-based" marketplace, thus an artist's website / interactive assets are pivotal to their marketing, branding and influence. South by Southwest's annual Interactive Web Awards along with the annual Webby Awards are among the elite awards that have music categories honoring Pandora, Pitchfork, NPR Music, Labuat and Echo & The Bunnymen in 2010 for the best and most innovative websites. In fact in 1999, The MTV Music Video Awards added the Best Artist Website category which was awarded to the Red Hot Chili Peppers only to remove the category shortly after. The GMA is posed to usher in the return of interactive acknowledgment to music industry awards, and given the above examples as evidence, show that the website design community-a multi-million dollar industry-is needed more than ever by professionals, artists and the fans.
> 
> We are exclusively proposing this plan to the Gospel Music Association, and encouraging them to consider the addition of the Interactive Marketing Website of the Year category.
> 
> Thank you for your consideration!

If you support and agree (or disagree) with my proposal, please comment on this post and write the GMA ([info@gospelmusic.org](<mailto:info@gospelmusic.org>)) urging them to add interactive efforts to the list of those recognized. More importantly, encourage them on the work they do to promote and advocate for Christian Music!
