![I Love My Commute](/content/uploads/2014/11/commute-300x200.jpg)I am often teased by my co-workers for living in Brentwood. Beyond the typical "you in the 'burbs" jokes, I get many comments from my teammates that live in East Nashville who think the idea of driving more than 10 minutes to work is outrageous. I have to admit -- on days like this morning, when the rain is coming down and 65N is a total zoo, there is some appeal to a quick "over the river" drive that prevents me from having to schedule to leave 45 minutes before I need to be at my desk.

But then I remember... _I love my commute_. Why? Because it is an amazing opportunity to spend an hour to an hour and a half each day reading books. No, I don't have one of the new drive-on-their-own Google cars (although if someone knows someone who can hook me up, let me know!). I have fallen in love with listening to audiobooks, which, despite many people's feelings to the contrary, I feel is just as valuable as reading the books themselves.

Let me take a step back. Somewhere around high school, I fell hard out of love with reading. Nothing made me disgusted more than the words " _required reading_ ," and all throughout high school and college I did as little reading as I possibly could. After college, when I no longer had a professor threatening to pop a quiz or assign a term paper, I was so turned off to reading that nothing could pique my interest enough to convince me to read for _pleasure_.

Fast forward a few years, when my commute to and from Centresource annoyed me. Even though I lived slightly closer at the time, my 45 min commute each day could not go quick enough. Radio ads were frustrating, and my music playlists grew stale quick. Around this time, podcasts started taking off and I, being a Mac and Gadget nerd, grew fond of "This Week in Tech" and "MacBreak Weekly." Before I knew it, I was sucked in to listening to the stories as I drove.

At one point, it clicked: _There are all of these books I hear are good, but haven't had the desire to read. What if maybe... just maybe... I listened to them during my commute?_ I signed up for a free Audible trial (offered through "This Week in Tech" -- thanks, Leo Laporte), and the rest is history.

Since 2010, I have completed almost 100 books -- over 35,000 pages that I otherwise would have never been exposed to, or had the desire to read. I've read all of the recent pop-culture series. I've read business books. I've read inspirational books. I've read biographies.

What's even better -- thanks to the amazing Whispersync technology that Amazon introduced that syncs the Kindle & Audible books, I can pick up my Kindle when I get home and have it be right where the audiobook left off.

I now consider one of my primary hobbies reading. I absolutely love reading and listening to books. I feel like I have learned how to read again.

...And I have my Centresource commute to thank for that.
