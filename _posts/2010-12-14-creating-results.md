The creative process is a balancing act.

Often, as artists we are caught in limbo somewhere between what we could do with an infinite amount of time and the reality of our imposed limitations.

As many of you know, we were recently nominated for Design Agency of the Year by .NET. As this was an exciting milestone for our agency, the pressure was on to create a promotion piece that was worthy of the nomination.

When we finally settled on a concept-a WWF Challenge video-we knew we had minimal time and resources to get it out the door.

So instead of getting caught up in the mire of perfectionism, we just got to work with what we had in front of us. We made the video in 30 mins with a flip cam, threw up a couple lights, star glasses, 80's rock, a fist full of attitude and we were in business. I grabbed my camera and snapped a few shots on the fly, then made a kind of over-the-top composite. It was all pretty fast.

Now, is the video and photo production perfect? No. But are they fun and interesting? Absolutely! Everyone has deadlines. There is a certain amount of time for every job we take as creatives and often the trick is to be awesome on the fly. Given enough time anyone can form the perfect solution. Time is a luxury we seldom have though.

It's easy for analysis paralysis to trap your mind into not getting things done. That is - thinking about taking action instead of actually taking action. But there are plenty of ways to conquer it. Change your perspective, read magazines & blogs, see what others have created, write your ideas on paper, mind-map, etc. One of the most effective ways I have found to be great at something is to simply do that thing all the time. Practice. The more you do, the more you learn as you go, the more it's just muscle memory. The goal is to be perfect, but while you are on the way, make something happen. Take the initiative to narrow your focus and execute your ideas. As the proverb goes, "A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush."

Having a tangible, completed idea is worth more than the possibility of better ideas you haven't thought of, or ideas you will never get around to executing.

So get a coffee. Make it happen.
