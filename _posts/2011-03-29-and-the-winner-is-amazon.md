You know that feeling of when you just started 10th grade and you don't know where to sit in the lunch room? I get the exact same feeling when I plug my ipod into a new computer and itunes gives me a warning saying "would you like to sync your ipod with this computer?". I get completely flustered and am afraid I'll make the wrong decision by erasing my whole prize collection of albums. Along the same line, remember Grooveshark? Remember how you had to "buffer" your music and sometimes it would delete your music? Today folks, Amazon has seemed to cure my worrisome, accidentally-deleting-my-music blues.

![](https://csblog.quietlife.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/GettingStarted_TCG._V184037123_11-300x228.png)

[Cloud Player](<http://www.amazon.com/b/ref=amb_link_355091782_4?ie=UTF8&node=2658409011&pf_rd_m=ATVPDKIKX0DER&pf_rd_s=center-2&pf_rd_r=1XCE7Q7BHQQA2C69H0SW&pf_rd_t=101&pf_rd_p=1291940422&pf_rd_i=163856011>) allows you to securely store your music, videos, photos, and documents online and access them from anywhere. The beauty of this platform is that all you need is a web browser to upload, download, and access your files from any computer without the hassle of plugging in an external mp3 player. You can also back up your files to ensure that your music, photos, and personal documents are available to you wherever you go.

Users are given 5 GB of free storage from the start up, but if you purchase an album from Amazon (even a $1.99 album) you get an extra 20 GB of music. It's $1 per GB after that. Its rumored that Google and Apple are hard at work creating their own 'Cloud' but it looks like Amazon beat them to the punch. I'm looking forward to seeing what they come up with but in the meantime, I'll be enjoying my music from the office and at home worry free.
