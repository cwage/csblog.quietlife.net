[![](/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/leadervmanager-300x175.png)](</wp-content/uploads/2012/07/leadervmanager.png> "Leader vs Manager")One of the opportunities I have on a weekly basis is to meet with leaders whom I deeply admire. These men and women are captains of industry. They have been through the ups and downs of the economy and came out strong. They have faced the difficult stress of keeping a family and company balanced. Over the past year I have kept a mental note of characteristics that make them unique. I've decided to call this series of blogs "Learning Leadership" as it will basically be a summary of what I'm learning as I learn it.

In this article I wanted to talk about the difference between leadership and management. Throughout most of my career, I've assumed these were the same role and that by assuming one of them, the other one was a requirement. I've learned that the two are actually quite different and unique. Also I've learned that being a good manager without being a great leader is, in fact, possible.

The difference is this:

**_While managers are often able to maintain a team at its current level, leaders lift the team to a higher level than it has ever reached before._**

Simply put, leaders are lifters. They push and pull. Managers walk with the pack. Both are vital roles within a company and neither is better than the other. We have this misconception that everyone should aspire to leadership when in fact some are best suited for management. With leadership, everything always needs to improve. With management, everything needs to stay functioning at its expected level.

As a leader, I am blessed to have an amazing team of leaders and managers on my team here at Centresource. Continuous improvement in an organization is critical, but must be managed. Constant improvement may lead to constant change. That, by definition, is not "change," but rather chaos. If nothing is constant, then it's not change...it's chaos.

So this is what I'm learning:

**People who manage to maintain status quo are equally as important as those that lead to raise the status quo.**

As long as the managers don't hold back the the leaders and the leaders don't run over the managers...greatness will be possible.
