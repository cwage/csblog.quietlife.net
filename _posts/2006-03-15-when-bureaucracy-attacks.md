We try to stay away from politics here on the centresource blog, but this is one area where they have affected security and technology, so I'll keep this neat and quick: One of the more amusing gifts we've been blessed with as a result of the PATRIOT Act is the set of "security" questions that Dell has to ask now, when you order goods from them.

They are rather amusing questions. My favorite one is "How do you intend to use this product?" The first time I was asked this, I was so taken aback that I just busted out laughing. "What do you mean how do I intend to use it? It's a laptop." These days my standard answer is "Computing." -- unless, of course, it's a printer I'm ordering, in which case I answer "Printing." I sometimes wonder what would happen if I answered "Violent overthrow of the government." I think it might involve a long vacation in gitmo.

This is the ultimate example of an attempt to maintain the illusion of security via worthless posturing -- I'm sure it makes [Bruce Schneier](<http://www.schneier.com/blog/>) proud. What good does this mandate actually do besides annoy other people and amuse me?
