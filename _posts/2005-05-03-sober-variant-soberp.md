There appears to be a variant of the Sober e-mail worm going around, [called Sober.P](<http://vil.nai.com/vil/content/v_133409.htm>). It seems to be going around rather quickly. We've had three different clients call us this morning about being overwhelmed by messages, and [Swirbo](<http://www.swirbo.com/>) has killed a few hundred instances of the worm today alone.

In response to this outbreak, a few steps can be taken:

  * Delete any messages from unfamiliar/suspicious senders with attachments. **Never** under any circumstances open unfamiliar attachments.
  * Check your automatic updates or visit [http://windowsupdate.microsoft.com](<http://windowsupdate.microsoft.com/>) to make sure your Windows PC has the latest updates.
  * Ensure that you Anti-Virus signatures are up-to-date.
  * If you think a PC might be infected, the McAfee [Stinger](<http://vil.nai.com/vil/stinger/>) tool can identify this new variant.



As always, clients of ours that are using [Swirbo](<http://www.swirbo.com/>) are protected from this new variant.
