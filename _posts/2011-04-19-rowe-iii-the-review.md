No ROWE system would be complete without a steady stream of updates about where you stand.

Afterall, one of the sweet parts of ROWE is the chance to take as much vacation as you can handle...as long as your 'R'esults have been met.

To get the feedback loop going, we first had to implement accurate data tracking for objective and subjective job performance criteria. Objectively, this goes back to the billable hour discussed last week. Subjectively, we need to have a central clearinghouse for any comments, be they positive or negative. These comments can come from co-workers, clients, or the managers here at CS. And honestly, most of the time these read like the things you'd say to yourself in the mirror to boost your confidence.

Yes, you are that good looking.

All of this info is shared with the team in a bi-monthly merit review. Each person gets to review their numbers, ask questions, give any feedback they might have, etc. No one likes more meetings, so we keep these to 15 minutes. In a good ROWE system there are no surprises for the staff or the managers. The only way to make sure that happens is to stay on the ball with the feedback loop. As an added bonus to the whole thing, we also started giving 'Badges' to our staff.

A Badge can be anything above and beyond the normal job role. When a Badge is rewarded, it comes with some cash and recognition. Every two weeks Nick walks around here like cash Santa, handing out high-fives and Fifties to the team members who received another Badge.

And that, as they say, is how it's done.
