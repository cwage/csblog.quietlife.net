![Content Calendar](/wp-content/uploads/2015/05/contentcal-300x261.png)Most salespeople enjoy reaping the rewards of incoming leads. Typically, these are leads that have done some research already and are ready to jump into the conversation. If these leads came naturally and consistently, we wouldn't need to think as much about PPC, SEO, social media and creating compelling content. However, people are looking for vendors by asking colleagues, reading publications and searching Google. Internally created content is valuable for generating leads, and establishing expertise around a topic. It's also not a bad idea to send your content to prospects and clients instead of constantly asking for new business... we've all done it.

An editorial content calendar allows collaboration around topics, assignment of responsibility, scheduling of release and determines the appropriate outlets for publishing content. Creating a content calendar is easy and can be updated weekly, monthly, or quarterly (that's our strategy).

It is best to have an internal champion for the content calendar, preferably someone in marketing that can also edit and provide insights into topics and channels. Once that champion is established, a simple Excel file works as your calendar.

Here are the necessary fields for your calendar:

  1. Topic (Keep a list of future topics so you can have people pick what they prefer to write about)
  2. Author
  3. Type of content (video, blog, infographic, podcast)
  4. Due date
  5. Date to be published
  6. Channels to publish to (Don't publish everything to every channel. Determine what your voice is on each social network and what it is on your blog, and what channel is best suited for each piece of content.)



Next step is to rinse, repeat, and enjoy some new leads.
