Ask anyone in Operations what keeps them up at night and they'll tell you it's losing control. Whether it stems from not having accurate data, miscalculations, or simple oversight, a lack of proper data driven understanding is [anathema](<http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/anathema>) in Operations departments. So you can imagine the cold fear that gripped myself and my Ops cohorts when Nick announced that Centresource would be going ROWE. 

For those of you who haven't read any of the latest buzz business blogs, ROWE simply means Results Only Work Environment. There are several variations, but the core tenet is that the only thing that matters for job performance are the results. So, if you are delivering said results, where and how you deliver them matter a whole lot less. This eliminates a lot of the 'be in the office by 8' and 'you only have 12 days of vacation this year' thinking that nearly all of us are used to. It is a brave new world. And for Operations it is also scary. 

Over the next few posts I will sketch out how we landed here, how we are measuring our results per team member, and some of the challenges we've faced along the way. It turns out that Ops and ROWE can co-exist. Tune in next time to find out how.
