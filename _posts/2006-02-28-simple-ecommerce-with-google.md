It looks like [Google is going to enter the eCommerce market](<https://web.archive.org/web/20060324052924/http://www.techcrunch.com/2006/02/27/exclusive-look-at-google-payments/>). My basic understanding is that Google will be allowing people to upload product information to Google Base and then when Google Payments is released they will be able to set a price for those items and accept payment.

There is no indication of how fulfillment will be handled, but it does sound like there will not be a transaction fee. I would imagine there is also not a need for a merchant bank account.

While this won't work for clients with larger eCommerce aspirations, it should be a decent option for entry level shopping cart services. Especially is there is a web service to interact with the store contents.
