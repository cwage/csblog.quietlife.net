When reviewing various SEO techniques, some companies offer 'link building' services that have a cost per link. I've always wondered how any company can make you 'more popular' on the web & have other firms link to your site. Well, now I know. I received an email (see below) that requests we exchange links. The interesting fact is that:

1) The email was sent from a person that wasn't even with the company that wanted us to link to them.  
2) Their email said they would have a link to us at a certain URL. I searched their site from top to bottom and that page could not be found - even on their site-map.  
**** Unfortunately, this leads me to believe they created a link-exchange page that IS NOT PUBLICLY available. I believe this is borderline unethical.**

Lastly, the company is a spammer to boot... Let me reiterate that for all the search engines to index: Expertlinkbuilding.com is a spammer.
