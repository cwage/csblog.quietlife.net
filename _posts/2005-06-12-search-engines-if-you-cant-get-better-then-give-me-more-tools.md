title: "Search Engines - If you can't get better, then give me more tools..."
date: 2005-06-12
---
I could post all day long on this, but I've really come to dislike search engines and the mentality that surrounds them. I dislike the engines themselves because they are not very good at giving me results. Studies galore show that 50% of the results often do not apply to your search query. As for the mentality, I dislike the fact that most people only look at that first page. Bad results + lazy searchers. So my proposal: If you can't give me better results, at least give me better tools to find them myself. I'm sure this is confusing, so read on for a better explanation.

I've always thought of search as an outside drill down. Take fore example: I need Computer Help in Nashville, TN. My ideal search engine would bring to a phase like approach. The first phase would be the verify the following:

1) You want to limit your results to Nashville, TN  
2) You want entries related to the specific word Computers  
3) You want entries related to the non-specific word Help

Wow, right off the bat we've really sliced away a lot of unnecessary data. In my ideal search engine, you'll have these criteria prominently listed on the initial results page - so I can edit/refine any of these. Now on the phase 2 - more drill down.

For now, I am happy with #1 (my location) of Nashville, TN. As for #2, I'm pretty sure I like it. But #3 is too general. Therefore, my ideal search engine helps me start there. Does 'Help' mean hiring an employee, finding a consultant, or taking a class. These are things my search engine would suggest. For this area, I would see these phrases - and even a list of synonyms for the word help. For my example, I choose 'I need a consultant'. Same as phase 1, now my phase 2 criteria are prominently displayed so I can go back and refine them if I need to.

At this point, I am at a cross roads. I can select from a list of results - or I can continue my drill down. Let's continue - and now we can focus on #2 (Computer). My ideal search engine would give me synonyms for Computer, as well as related phrases - perhaps those that are the most popular searches, etc. For phase 3, I can choose items such as Computer Hardware, Computer Software, Computer Security, Computer Training. It would even be a great feature to allow me to click check-boxes next to each one that might apply - and then segment them in the results window. For this scenario, I choose both Computer Security and Computer Hardware. The results page automatically updates and my refined criteria are easily displayed so I can update them.

So I look over my results... and I start to notice things that don't belong. In my results, I am seeing many entries for 'Y2K' consultants. Since I want to get rid of them, I have an area (right there on the results page) where I can enter in 'Negative' words - things I want to be filtered out. While I know some search engines have negative words, its always an 'initial' item - and rarely do you know the negative terms until you get your search results.

And now for the clincher... the icing on the cake... the grand finale!

I want to save this search, and then perform SUB-SEARCHES on it! Now that I have a list of all Computer Consultant in Nashville, TN that perform security and hardware... now I want to see which one also offers Web Design services! And I want to do that without diluting my original search!!! If this all worked out correctly, then I could easily locate [http://www.centresource.com](<https://web.archive.org/web/20050615014726/http://www.centresource.com/>) \- and it wouldn't be merely based on the fact that CentreSource was lucky enough to show up on the home page for a single search phrase.

I understand why services can't keep up with directories - but you have to admit that directories have a lot of good features in them... especially the absence of a lot of junk.
